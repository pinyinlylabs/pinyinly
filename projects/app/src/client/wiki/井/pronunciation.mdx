**🗣️ Pronunciation of 井 (jǐng)**

- **Pinyin:** jǐng
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **ǐng** sounds like **"ing"** in "sing", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **jǐng** sounds like **"jing"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is a **fall–then-rise** tone, like looking down into something deep:

Say it like you're peering thoughtfully into a deep well: **"jǐng..."** — that contemplative
dip-and-rise is the **third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 井 (jǐng) - "well; water well"
- 水井 (shuǐ jǐng) - "water well"
- 油井 (yóu jǐng) - "oil well"
- 井水 (jǐng shuǐ) - "well water"
- 深井 (shēn jǐng) - "deep well"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **井** as looking down thoughtfully into a deep well — that's the **third tone**
downward-then-upward movement!

**📍 Visual Connection:**

The character 井 actually looks like the grid pattern you might see when looking down into a
traditional well with wooden supports!
