**🗣️ Pronunciation of 矛 (máo)**

- **Pinyin:** máo
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "me"
- **áo** sounds like **"ow"** in "cow" but with a rising tone
- **máo** sounds like **"mow?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're questioning: **"máo?"** — that's the rising tone pattern of **máo**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 矛 (máo) - "spear; lance"
- 矛盾 (máo dùn) - "contradiction" (literally "spear and shield")
- 长矛 (cháng máo) - "long spear; pike"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **"mow?"** with a questioning rise — when someone mentions a **spear**, you might ask
"máo?" to confirm what weapon they're talking about!

**📝 Cultural Note:**

矛 is most commonly seen in the phrase 矛盾 (máo dùn), which literally means "spear and shield" but
figuratively means "contradiction" — from an ancient story about a merchant who claimed to sell both
an unstoppable spear and an impenetrable shield.
