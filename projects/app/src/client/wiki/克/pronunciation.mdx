**🗣️ Pronunciation of 克 (kè)**

- **Pinyin:** kè
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **k** like **"k"** in "kite"
- **è** sounds like **"uh"** in "duh" but with a sharp falling tone
- **kè** sounds like **"kuh!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're giving a firm command: **"kè!"** — that's the tone pattern of **kè**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 克 (kè) - "gram" (unit of measurement)
- 千克 (qiān kè) - "kilogram"
- 克服 (kè fú) - "to overcome"
- 巧克力 (qiǎo kè lì) - "chocolate"

**💡 Special Notes:**

克 is commonly used as a unit of measurement (gram) and also appears in words meaning "to overcome"
or "to conquer." The pronunciation remains consistently fourth tone (kè) across all uses.
