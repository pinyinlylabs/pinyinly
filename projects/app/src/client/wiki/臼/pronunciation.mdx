**🗣️ Pronunciation of 臼 (jiù)**

- **Pinyin:** jiù
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like stating something firmly

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep" (soft j sound)
- **iù** sounds like **"yo"** in "yo-yo", but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **jiù** sounds like **"jyo"** with a definitive falling tone

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it with conviction, like saying **"There!"** when placing something: **"jiù!"** — sharp and
final.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 臼 (jiù) - "mortar (for grinding)"
- 石臼 (shí jiù) - "stone mortar"
- 臼齿 (jiù chǐ) - "molar tooth"

**💡 Cultural Note:**

臼 (jiù) refers to a traditional stone or wooden mortar used for grinding grains, spices, or other
materials. It's also used as a radical in Chinese characters and can refer to molar teeth due to
their grinding function.
