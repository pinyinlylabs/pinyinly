Describes something that is formal or relates to official matters; formal; official.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                 |
| -------------- | -------------------- |
| Pinyin         | zhèng shì           |
| Core meaning   | formal; official     |
| Part of speech | adjective            |
| Tone           | 4th + 4th tone       |

## Visual Breakdown

正式 combines **correct + ceremony** to represent official, proper formality.

| Component | Meaning                     | Contribution to 正式                      |
| --------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **正**    | correct; straight; proper   | Shows adherence to proper standards      |
| **式**    | ceremony; style; formula    | Emphasizes structured, ceremonial nature |

## Character Analysis: 正

正 shows **a line (一) over a base (止)**:

- **一** (line) represents a straight, correct line
- **止** (foot/base) shows a firm foundation
- Together: standing straight and correct on a solid foundation

## Character Analysis: 式

式 depicts **work (工) + ceremony (弋)**:

- **工** (work/craft) represents skilled, proper execution
- **弋** originally meant ceremony or ritual
- Together: skillfully executed ceremony or formal procedure

## Mnemonic

Think of 正式 as **"straight ceremony"**:

- 正 (correct) represents doing things the proper, straight way
- 式 (ceremony) shows following established formal procedures
- Picture a formal ceremony where everyone stands straight and follows proper protocol
- Like a graduation ceremony - everything must be done correctly and formally

## Usage Examples

- **正式开始** (zhèng shì kāi shǐ) - "formally begin"
- **正式会议** (zhèng shì huì yì) - "formal meeting"
- **正式服装** (zhèng shì fú zhuāng) - "formal attire"
- **正式邀请** (zhèng shì yāo qǐng) - "formal invitation"
- **正式工作** (zhèng shì gōng zuò) - "formal job"

## Grammar Patterns

正式 is used as:

- **Adjective**: 正式的 + [noun] - "formal [noun]"
- **Adverb**: 正式 + [verb] - "formally [verb]"
- **Contrast**: 不正式 - "informal"

## Common Expressions

- **正式场合** (zhèng shì chǎng hé) - "formal occasion"
- **正式通知** (zhèng shì tōng zhī) - "formal notice"
- **正式员工** (zhèng shì yuán gōng) - "formal employee"

## Cultural Context

正式 reflects Chinese social structure:

- **Protocol importance**: The significance of following proper procedures
- **Hierarchy respect**: Formal behavior shows respect for authority and tradition
- **Social harmony**: Formality helps maintain order and predictability
- **Professional standards**: The importance of maintaining professional appearance and behavior
