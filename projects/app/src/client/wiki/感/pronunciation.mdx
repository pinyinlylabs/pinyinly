**🗣️ Pronunciation of 感 (gǎn)**

- **Pinyin:** gǎn
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like feeling something deeply

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **g** like **"g"** in "go"
- **ǎn** sounds like **"ahn"** with third tone → dip down and rise up, with nasal ending
- **gǎn** sounds like **"gahn"** with a contemplative dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're feeling something deep inside: **"gǎn..."** — that contemplative dip-and-rise
captures the process of sensing or experiencing something.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 感 (gǎn) - "to feel; to sense; feeling"
- 感觉 (gǎn jué) - "to feel; feeling; sensation"
- 感谢 (gǎn xiè) - "to thank; grateful"
- 感动 (gǎn dòng) - "to move; to touch emotionally"
- 感冒 (gǎn mào) - "cold; flu"
- 感受 (gǎn shòu) - "to feel; to experience"

**🎭 Usage Note:**

感 (gǎn) is central to expressing sensations, emotions, and responses. It often combines with other
characters to form words about physical feelings, emotional states, and reactions.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of the way you say "hmm..." when you're trying to feel or sense something — **"gǎn..."** —
that thoughtful, dipping tone perfectly captures the act of feeling!
