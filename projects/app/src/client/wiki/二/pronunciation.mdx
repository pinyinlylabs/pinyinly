**🗣️ Pronunciation of 二 (èr)**

- **Pinyin:** èr
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Stop!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **è** sounds like **"ar"** in "art" but with a sharp falling tone
- **r** is a slight **"r"** sound, but softer than English
- **èr** sounds like **"ar!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip (Fourth tone: ˋ)**

The **fourth tone** is a sharp **falling** tone, like being definitive:

Say it like you're counting firmly: **"èr!"** — that's the decisive tone of **èr**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 二 (èr) - "two"
- 二十 (èr shí) - "twenty"
- 第二 (dì èr) - "second"
- 二月 (èr yuè) - "February"
- 星期二 (xīng qī èr) - "Tuesday"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **二** as the definitive number two — that's the **fourth tone** certainty!

**📍 Note on Usage:**

二 (èr) is the formal/literary way to say "two." In spoken Chinese, 两 (liǎng) is more commonly used
when counting objects, but 二 is used in numbers, dates, and formal contexts.
