**🗣️ Pronunciation of 茶 (chá)**

- **Pinyin:** chá
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "charm"
- **á** sounds like **"ah"** but with second tone → rise up
- **chá** sounds like **"chah"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ´)**

The **second tone** is a **rising** tone, like asking a question:

Say it like you're offering tea: **"chá?"** — that questioning rise is the **second tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 茶 (chá) - "tea"
- 喝茶 (hē chá) - "to drink tea"
- 茶叶 (chá yè) - "tea leaves"
- 绿茶 (lǜ chá) - "green tea"
- 红茶 (hóng chá) - "black tea"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **茶** as politely offering tea with an upward, welcoming tone — that hospitable rise
matches the **second tone**!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

茶 is central to Chinese culture with thousands of years of tea tradition. Tea ceremony, different
tea varieties, and tea as a social drink are deeply embedded in Chinese social customs.
