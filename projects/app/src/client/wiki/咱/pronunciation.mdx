**🗣️ Pronunciation of 咱 (zán)**

- **Pinyin:** zán
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **z** like **"ds"** in "adds", but as a single sound
- **án** sounds like **"ahn"** but with a rising tone
- **zán** sounds like **"dzahn?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking a question: **"zán?"** — let your voice rise at the end.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 咱 (zán) - "we; us" (inclusive)
- 咱们 (zán men) - "we; us" (inclusive)
- 咱家 (zán jiā) - "our family/home"
- 咱俩 (zán liǎ) - "we two; both of us"

**💡 Usage Note:**

咱 (zán) is more **colloquial and inclusive** than 我们 (wǒ men):

- **咱们** includes the listener ("we" including you)
- **我们** may or may not include the listener ("we" possibly excluding you)

咱 is commonly used in northern China dialects and casual speech.
