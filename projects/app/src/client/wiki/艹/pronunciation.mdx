**🗣️ Pronunciation of 艹 (cǎo)**

- **Pinyin:** cǎo
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **c** like **"ts"** in "cats"
- **ǎo** sounds like **"ow"** but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **cǎo** sounds like **"tsao"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is a **fall–then-rise** tone, like being thoughtful:

Say it like you're thinking about grass: **"cǎo..."** — that thoughtful dip-and-rise is the **third
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 艹 (cǎo) - "grass radical"
- 草 (cǎo) - "grass" (uses this radical)
- 花 (huā) - "flower" (uses this radical)
- 茶 (chá) - "tea" (uses this radical)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **艹** as the grass radical swaying in the wind — that up-and-down motion matches the
**third tone**!

**📍 Special Note:**

艹 is the "grass radical" that appears at the top of many plant-related characters. When written at
the top of characters, it's often simplified to three short strokes (艹), but when standalone, it
can be written as 草.
