**🗣️ Pronunciation of 夫 (fū)**

- **Pinyin:** fū
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Foo"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **f** like **"f"** in "food"
- **ū** sounds like **"oo"** in "book" but held steady and high
- **fū** sounds like **"foo"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're respectfully addressing a man: **"fū..."** — that steady, dignified tone is the
**first tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 夫 (fū) - "man; husband"
- 夫人 (fū rén) - "wife; madam"
- 大夫 (dài fū) - "doctor"
- 工夫 (gōng fū) - "time; effort; kung fu"
- 夫妇 (fū fù) - "married couple"
- 农夫 (nóng fū) - "farmer"

**💡 Special Notes:**

夫 traditionally refers to an adult man, particularly in the context of marriage (husband) or as a
respectful term for men in certain professions. Note that in 大夫 (doctor), the pronunciation
changes to "dài fū" where 大 is pronounced with the fourth tone. The character also appears in
compound words related to work and time.
