**🗣️ Pronunciation of 舛 (chuǎn)**

- **Pinyin:** chuǎn
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when thinking

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "choose"
- **uǎn** sounds like **"wan"** in "want", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **chuǎn** sounds like **"chwan"** with a thoughtful dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're considering something contradictory: **"chuǎn..."** — that contemplative dip and
rise.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 舛 (chuǎn) - "contradictory; inconsistent"
- 舛错 (chuǎn cuò) - "error; mistake"
- 舛驳 (chuǎn bó) - "contradictory; inconsistent"

**💡 Usage Note:**

舛 (chuǎn) is a formal/literary character meaning "contradictory" or "inconsistent." It's not
commonly used in everyday speech but appears in written Chinese and formal contexts.
