**🗣️ Pronunciation of 谷 (gǔ)**

- **Pinyin:** gǔ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **g** like **"g"** in "go"
- **ǔ** sounds like **"oo"** in "book" but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **gǔ** sounds like **"goo"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is **falling-rising**, like when you're contemplating:

Say it like you're thinking about a peaceful valley: **"gǔ..."** — that dip-and-rise pattern is the
**third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 谷 (gǔ) - "valley; grain"
- 山谷 (shān gǔ) - "mountain valley"
- 谷物 (gǔ wù) - "grain; cereal"
- 峡谷 (xiá gǔ) - "canyon; gorge"
- 河谷 (hé gǔ) - "river valley"
- 五谷 (wǔ gǔ) - "five grains" (traditional term for staple crops)

**💡 Dual Meaning:**

谷 (gǔ) has two main meanings:

1. **Geographic**: valley, ravine
2. **Agricultural**: grain, cereal crops

Both meanings share the same pronunciation and are related to the concept of fertile, productive
low-lying areas.
