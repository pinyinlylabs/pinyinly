**🗣️ Pronunciation of 匸 (xǐ)**

- **Pinyin:** xǐ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **x** like **"sh"** in "she" (but with tongue tip down)
- **ǐ** sounds like **"ee"** in "see", but with the **third tone** → dip down and rise up
- **xǐ** sounds like **"shee"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're hesitantly considering: **"xǐ?"** — that uncertain, questioning tone pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 匸 (xǐ) - "hiding enclosure" (radical)
- 匹 (pǐ) - contains this radical
- 区 (qū) - historically related to this radical

**💡 Memory Tip:**

匸 means "hiding enclosure" — the uncertain third tone sounds like you're hesitantly looking for
something hidden!

**📝 Note:**

匸 is primarily used as a radical component in other characters and is not commonly used
independently in modern Chinese.
