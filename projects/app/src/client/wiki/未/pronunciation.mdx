**🗣️ Pronunciation of 未 (wèi)**

- **Pinyin:** wèi
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **èi** sounds like **"ay"** in "way", but with fourth tone → sharp downward fall
- **wèi** sounds like **"way!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're making a definitive statement: **"wèi!"** — that firm downward drop is the fourth
tone pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 未 (wèi) - "not yet, have not"
- 未来 (wèi lái) - "future"
- 未必 (wèi bì) - "not necessarily"
- 尚未 (shàng wèi) - "not yet, still not"
- 未曾 (wèi céng) - "never, not ever"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **未** as firmly stating "not yet" — the decisive falling tone matches the definitive
nature of something that hasn't happened yet!
