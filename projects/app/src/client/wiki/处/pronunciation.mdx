**🗣️ Pronunciation of 处 (chù)**

- **Pinyin:** chù
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Choo!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "church" (but with tongue curled back)
- **ù** sounds like **"oo"** in "book" but with a sharp falling tone
- **chù** sounds like **"choo"** with a decisive drop

**🎯 Mastering the Chinese "ch" sound:**

The **ch** in Chinese has **retroflex tongue position**:

1. **Curl tongue back** — tip pointing toward roof of mouth
2. **Make "ch" sound** — air puffs around curled tongue
3. **Aspirated** — strong puff of air follows
4. **Deeper than English** — richer resonance

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is **authoritative and falling**:

Start **high and drop decisively** — like pointing to a location: **"chù!"** (there!)

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 处 (chù) - "place"
- 处理 (chù lǐ) - "handle"
- 到处 (dào chù) - "everywhere"
- 好处 (hǎo chù) - "benefit"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of pointing decisively to a **place** — the sharp falling fourth tone is like firmly
indicating **"chù!"** (right there!)
