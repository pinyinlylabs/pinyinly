**🗣️ Pronunciation of 典 (diǎn)**

- **Pinyin:** diǎn
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "day"
- **iǎn** sounds like **"yen"** but with the **third tone** → dip down and rise up
- **diǎn** sounds like **"d-yen"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're being thoughtful or considering something: **"diǎn..."** — that's the tone
pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 典 (diǎn) - "document; classic"
- 典型 (diǎn xíng) - "typical"
- 字典 (zì diǎn) - "dictionary"
- 经典 (jīng diǎn) - "classic"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of flipping through pages of a classic document — your voice "dips and rises" like turning
pages!
