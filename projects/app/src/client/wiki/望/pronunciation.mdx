**🗣️ Pronunciation of 望 (wàng)**

- **Pinyin:** wàng
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **àng** sounds like **"ahng"** in "gong", but with fourth tone → sharp downward fall
- **wàng** sounds like **"wahng"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're making a firm statement: **"wàng!"** — that decisive drop is the fourth tone
pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 望 (wàng) - "to look at, to gaze"
- 希望 (xī wàng) - "hope"
- 望远镜 (wàng yuǎn jìng) - "telescope"
- 失望 (shī wàng) - "disappointed"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **望** as "looking" with determination — the sharp falling tone matches the decisive action
of looking or hoping for something!
