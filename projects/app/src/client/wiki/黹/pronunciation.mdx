**🗣️ Pronunciation of 黹 (zhǐ)**

- **Pinyin:** zhǐ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **zh** like **"j"** in "judge" but with tongue curled back
- **ǐ** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **zhǐ** sounds like **"jee"** with a dip-then-rise, similar to "gee" but with the retroflex "zh"
  sound

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thoughtfully examining embroidery work: **"zhǐ..."** — that contemplative tone
pattern when appreciating detailed needlework.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 黹 (zhǐ) - "embroidery; needlework"
- 黹工 (zhǐ gōng) - "embroidery work"
- 黹绣 (zhǐ xiù) - "embroidery and needlework"
