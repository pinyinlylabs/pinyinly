**🗣️ Pronunciation of 东 (dōng)**

- **Pinyin:** dōng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Dong"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "dog"
- **ōng** sounds like **"ong"** in "song", but with first tone → high and steady
- **dōng** sounds like **"dong"** with a high, flat pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're holding a steady note: **"dōng..."** — that's the tone pattern of **dōng**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 东 (dōng) - "east"
- 东方 (dōng fāng) - "the East; Orient"
- 东西 (dōng xi) - "thing; stuff" (note: different meaning!)
- 东北 (dōng běi) - "northeast"
- 东南 (dōng nán) - "southeast"

**💡 Special Notes:**

东 means "east" and is one of the four cardinal directions. Interestingly, 东西 (dōng xi) doesn't
mean "east-west" but rather "thing" or "stuff" in everyday conversation. The character is
fundamental for directions, geography, and cultural references to Eastern vs. Western concepts.
