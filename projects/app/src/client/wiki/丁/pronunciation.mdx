**🗣️ Pronunciation of 丁 (dīng)**

- **Pinyin:** dīng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Ding"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "dog"
- **īng** sounds like **"ing"** in "sing", but with first tone → high and steady
- **dīng** sounds like **"ding"** with a high, flat pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're holding a steady note: **"dīng..."** — that's the tone pattern of **dīng**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 丁 (dīng) - "4th (in sequence)"
- 丁字路 (dīng zì lù) - "T-junction"
- 园丁 (yuán dīng) - "gardener"

**💡 Special Notes:**

丁 is commonly used as an ordinal marker meaning "fourth" in traditional Chinese sequencing
(甲乙丙丁). It can also mean "male adult" or appear in compound words related to people or
professions.
