**🗣️ Pronunciation of 禸 (róu)**

- **Pinyin:** róu
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **r** like **"r"** in "red" (but softer, more like a light "zh" sound)
- **óu** sounds like **"oh"** but with second tone → rise up
- **róu** sounds like **"roe"** with a rising tone

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking: **"róu?"** — that's the questioning rise of **róu**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 禸 (róu) - "tracks; footprints" (rarely used independently)

**💡 Note on Usage:**

禸 is primarily used as a radical component in other characters and is rarely used as a standalone
character in modern Chinese. It represents the concept of tracks or animal footprints and appears in
characters related to movement or traces.
