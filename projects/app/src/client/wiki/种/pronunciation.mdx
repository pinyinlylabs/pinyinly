**🗣️ Pronunciation of 种 (zhǒng)**

- **Pinyin:** zhǒng
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **zh** like **"j"** in "judge" but with tongue tip curled back
- **ǒng** sounds like **"ong"** in "song", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **zhǒng** sounds like **"jong"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking or being thoughtful: **"zhǒng..."** — that's the contemplative
dip-and-rise of **zhǒng**.

**📝 Note on Pronunciation:**

种 has two pronunciations:

- **种 (zhǒng)** - "type; kind; species" (third tone)
- **种 (zhòng)** - "to plant; to grow" (fourth tone)

**📝 Common Examples with zhǒng:**

- 种 (zhǒng) - "type; kind"
- 各种 (gè zhǒng) - "various types"
- 种子 (zhǒng zi) - "seed"
- 种族 (zhǒng zú) - "race; ethnicity"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

For the "type/kind" meaning, think of the third tone's thoughtful quality — you're contemplating
different **types** of things.
