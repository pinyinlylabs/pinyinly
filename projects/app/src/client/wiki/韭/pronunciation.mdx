**🗣️ Pronunciation of 韭 (jiǔ)**

- **Pinyin:** jiǔ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **iǔ** sounds like **"yo"** in "yo-yo", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **jiǔ** sounds like **"joe"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone. Say **"jiǔ"** like you're thinking or being
thoughtful about the leek you're looking at.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 韭 (jiǔ) - "leek"
- 韭菜 (jiǔ cài) - "Chinese chives/leeks"
- 韭黄 (jiǔ huáng) - "blanched Chinese chives"
- 韭菜盒子 (jiǔ cài hé zi) - "leek dumpling"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of thoughtfully saying "**Joe**" when examining a leek — that dip-and-rise tone pattern is
**jiǔ**!
