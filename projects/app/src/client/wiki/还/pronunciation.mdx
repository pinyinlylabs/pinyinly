**🗣️ Pronunciation of 还**

还 has **two different pronunciations** depending on its meaning:

**📍 hái (second tone) - "still, also, even more"**

- **Pinyin:** hái
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **h** like **"h"** in "hat"
- **ái** sounds like **"eye"** but with a rising tone
- **hái** sounds like **"hi?"** with a questioning rise

**📍 huán (second tone) - "to return, to give back"**

- **Pinyin:** huán
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, same as above

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **h** like **"h"** in "hat"
- **uán** sounds like **"wan"** in "want" but with a rising tone
- **huán** sounds like **"hwan?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

Both pronunciations use **second tone** (ˊ) - a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're surprised or asking a question — that's the energy of both **hái** and **huán**.

**📝 Usage Guide:**

**还 (hái) - "still, also, even more" (most common):**

- 我**还**要 (wǒ hái yào) - "I still want"
- **还**有 (hái yǒu) - "there's also/still"
- **还**可以 (hái kě yǐ) - "still okay/pretty good"
- **还**是 (hái shì) - "or/still"
- 比这个**还**好 (bǐ zhè ge hái hǎo) - "even better than this"

**还 (huán) - "to return, to give back":**

- **还**钱 (huán qián) - "to pay back money"
- **还**书 (huán shū) - "to return books"
- 归**还** (guī huán) - "to return/give back"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**hái** = "hi, still here!" — continuing, persisting **huán** = "here, wanna return this?" — giving
back
