**🗣️ Pronunciation of 斗 (dǒu)**

- **Pinyin:** dǒu
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "dog"
- **ǒu** sounds like **"oh"** in "oh no" but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **dǒu** sounds like **"doh"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're pondering a measurement — **"dǒu..."** — that thoughtful dip-and-rise is the
**third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 斗 (dǒu) - "measurement unit" (traditional Chinese measure)
- 漏斗 (lòu dǒu) - "funnel"
- 北斗 (běi dǒu) - "Big Dipper"
- 斗量 (dǒu liáng) - "to measure by the dou"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **斗** as an ancient measuring cup — you hold it thoughtfully (third tone) when measuring
grain or rice.

**📍 Cultural Note:**

斗 was historically used as a unit of volume measurement in ancient China, equivalent to about 10
liters. It's still used in traditional contexts and idioms.
