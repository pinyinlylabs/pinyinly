A matter requiring an answer or solution; question; problem; issue; difficulty.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                             |
| -------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | wèntí                            |
| Core meaning   | question; problem; issue; matter |
| Part of speech | noun                             |
| Tone           | wèn (4th), tí (2nd)              |

## Word Breakdown

问题 combines concepts of questioning and topic to represent matters needing attention.

| Component | Meaning                                             |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **问**    | Ask; question; inquire - representing investigation |
| **题**    | Topic; subject; title - representing the matter     |

Together they create: "questioned topic" or "matter of inquiry" - something that needs to be
addressed or solved.

## Mnemonic

Think of 问题 as **"a topic that raises questions and needs investigation"**:

- 问 (wèn) represents asking, questioning, and seeking answers
- 题 (tí) shows the topic, subject, or matter at hand
- Together: a subject matter that prompts questions and requires attention
- Like a headline that makes you ask "What's going on?"
- The combination of curiosity and a specific focus area

This creates the image: **a subject matter that demands questioning and investigation**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

问题 represents **questions, problems, and matters requiring attention**. It's used:

1. **Questions**: 有问题吗 (yǒu wèntí ma) - "any questions?"
2. **Problems**: 解决问题 (jiějué wèntí) - "solve problems"
3. **Issues**: 讨论问题 (tǎolùn wèntí) - "discuss issues"
4. **Matters**: 重要问题 (zhòngyào wèntí) - "important matters"

## Examples

- **没问题** (méi wèntí) - "no problem; no question"
- **有问题** (yǒu wèntí) - "there's a problem; have questions"
- **问题很多** (wèntí hěnduō) - "many problems"
- **解决问题** (jiějué wèntí) - "solve problems"
- **提出问题** (tíchū wèntí) - "raise questions"

## Cultural Context

问题 in Chinese thinking represents:

**Problem-Solving Values:**

- **实事求是** (shíshì qiúshì) - Seek truth from facts
- **积极解决** (jījí jiějué) - Actively solve problems
- **预防为主** (yùfáng wéi zhǔ) - Prevention is primary
- **团队合作** (tuánduì hézuò) - Teamwork in problem-solving

**Learning Approach:**

- **勤学好问** (qínxué hàowèn) - Study diligently and ask questions
- **打破沙锅问到底** - Ask questions until getting to the bottom
- **举一反三** (jǔ yī fǎn sān) - Draw inferences from one example

## Common Expressions

- **问题不大** (wèntí bù dà) - "not a big problem"
- **问题严重** (wèntí yánzhòng) - "the problem is serious"
- **问题的关键** (wèntí de guānjiàn) - "the key to the problem"
- **问题在于** (wèntí zàiyú) - "the problem lies in"

## Grammar Functions

- **Subject**: 问题很复杂 (wèntí hěn fùzá) - "the problem is complex"
- **Object**: 回答问题 (huídá wèntí) - "answer questions"
- **With measure words**: 一个问题 (yī gè wèntí) - "one problem"

## Solution-Oriented Language

- **突破问题** (tūpò wèntí) - "break through problems"

## Learning Notes

问题 is essential because:

- Fundamental concept for inquiry, learning, and problem-solving
- Essential for academic, professional, and personal communication
- Key to expressing challenges and seeking help
- Important for critical thinking and analysis
- Demonstrates how compound words express complex concepts

问题 reflects the Chinese understanding that both questions and problems represent opportunities for
investigation, learning, and growth through careful analysis and solution-seeking!
