**🗣️ Pronunciation of 辆 (liàng)**

- **Pinyin:** liàng
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **l** like **"l"** in "light"
- **iàng** sounds like **"yang"** but with fourth tone → sharp falling
- **liàng** sounds like **"l-yang!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're definitively counting vehicles: **"liàng!"** — that's the tone pattern of
**liàng**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 辆 (liàng) - "measure word for vehicles"
- 一辆车 (yī liàng chē) - "one car"
- 几辆 (jǐ liàng) - "several vehicles"
- 两辆 (liǎng liàng) - "two vehicles"
- 这辆 (zhè liàng) - "this vehicle"

**📍 Usage Note:**

辆 is a **measure word** specifically used for counting vehicles like cars, trucks, bicycles, etc.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

辆 is for counting "vehicles" — the **sharp falling** tone is like the decisive act of pointing to
each vehicle as you count!
