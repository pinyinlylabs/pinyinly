**🗣️ Pronunciation of 段 (duàn)**

- **Pinyin:** duàn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "do"
- **uàn** sounds like **"wan"** but with the **fourth tone** → sharp fall down
- **duàn** sounds like **"dwan"** with a sharp dropping pitch

**🎧 Tone tip (Fourth tone: ˋ)**

The **fourth tone** is **sharp and falling**, like making a firm statement:

Say it like you're decisively marking off a section: **"duàn!"** — that sharp, falling tone is the
**fourth tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 段 (duàn) - "paragraph; section; segment"
- 段落 (duàn luò) - "paragraph"
- 阶段 (jiē duàn) - "stage; phase"
- 手段 (shǒu duàn) - "method; means"
- 一段时间 (yī duàn shí jiān) - "a period of time"

**💡 Special Notes:**

段 is commonly used to refer to divisions or sections of text, time, or physical objects. It's
frequently used in academic and formal writing when discussing paragraphs or sections of documents.
