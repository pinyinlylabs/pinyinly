**🗣️ Pronunciation of 九 (jiǔ)**

- **Pinyin:** jiǔ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **iǔ** sounds like **"yo"** in "yo-yo" but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **jiǔ** sounds like **"jyo"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're counting thoughtfully — **"jiǔ..."** — taking your time with the number nine.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 九 (jiǔ) - "nine"
- 九月 (jiǔ yuè) - "September"
- 九十 (jiǔ shí) - "ninety"
- 九点 (jiǔ diǎn) - "nine o'clock"

**📍 Cultural Note:**

九 is considered a **lucky number** in Chinese culture because it sounds similar to 久 (jiǔ) meaning
**"long time, lasting"**.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Nine** with the thoughtful third tone — like you're carefully counting up to the final single
digit!
