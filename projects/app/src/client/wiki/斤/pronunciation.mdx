**🗣️ Pronunciation of 斤 (jīn)**

- **Pinyin:** jīn
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Jeen"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **īn** sounds like **"een"** in "seen" but with first tone → high and steady
- **jīn** sounds like **"jeen"** with a high, flat pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're stating a weight precisely: **"jīn..."** — that's the steady, confident tone
pattern of **jīn**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 斤 (jīn) - "jin" (500 grams, Chinese catty)
- 公斤 (gōng jīn) - "kilogram"
- 半斤 (bàn jīn) - "half a jin" (250 grams)
- 三斤 (sān jīn) - "three jin" (1.5 kg)
- 几斤 (jǐ jīn) - "how many jin?"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Jin** with the steady first tone — like confidently stating the weight at a market!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

斤 is still commonly used in Chinese markets and cooking. One 斤 equals 500 grams or 1.1 pounds.
It's essential for shopping for groceries and understanding recipes in Chinese.
