**🗣️ Pronunciation of 豕 (shǐ)**

- **Pinyin:** shǐ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "she"
- **ǐ** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **shǐ** sounds like **"shee"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is **falling-rising**, like when you're thinking:

Say it like you're contemplating: **"shǐ..."** — that dip-and-rise pattern is the **third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 豕 (shǐ) - "pig; swine" (classical/literary term)

**💡 Character Note:**

豕 (shǐ) is primarily used as a **radical** in Chinese characters and appears in classical texts. In
modern Chinese, 猪 (zhū) is the common word for "pig." However, 豕 appears in various characters
related to pigs or animals:

- 家 (jiā) - "home; family" (contains 豕 radical)
- 豪 (háo) - "bold; heroic" (contains 豕 radical)

**🏛️ Historical Context:**

豕 (shǐ) is an ancient character that represents the pictograph of a pig. While not commonly used in
everyday modern Chinese, it's important for understanding character composition and classical texts.
