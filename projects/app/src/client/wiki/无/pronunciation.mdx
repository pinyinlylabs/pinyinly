**🗣️ Pronunciation of 无 (wú)**

- **Pinyin:** wú
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **ú** sounds like **"oo"** in "moon" but with second tone → rising tone
- **wú** sounds like **"woo?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're expressing surprise at the absence of something: **"wú?"** — that rising tone
captures the questioning nature of "nothing there?"

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 无 (wú) - "not have, without, none"
- 无法 (wú fǎ) - "unable to, no way to"
- 无聊 (wú liáo) - "boring, bored"
- 无论 (wú lùn) - "regardless of, no matter"
- 无所谓 (wú suǒ wèi) - "doesn't matter, indifferent"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

When you discover there's **nothing** there, your voice rises in surprise — just like the rising
second tone of **wú**!

**📍 Usage Note:**

无 is a versatile negation character, often used in formal or literary contexts. It's more formal
than 没有 (not have) in everyday speech.
