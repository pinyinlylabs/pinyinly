**🗣️ Pronunciation of 皿 (mǐn)**

- **Pinyin:** mǐn
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "me"
- **ǐn** sounds like **"een"** in "seen", but with the **third tone** → dip down and rise up
- **mǐn** sounds like **"mean"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thoughtfully considering a dish: **"mǐn..."** — that contemplative dip-then-rise
pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 皿 (mǐn) - "dish; vessel" (radical)
- 盘 (pán) - "plate; tray" (contains 皿)
- 盆 (pén) - "basin; pot" (contains 皿)
- 益 (yì) - "benefit; profit" (contains 皿)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

皿 represents "dish" or "vessel" — the dip-then-rise third tone sounds like someone carefully
examining a beautiful dish!

**📝 Note:**

皿 is primarily used as a radical component in characters related to containers, dishes, and
vessels. It's not commonly used independently in modern Chinese.
