**🗣️ Pronunciation of 太 (tài)**

- **Pinyin:** tài
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — **falling** tone, like stating a fact: **"Tie!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **t** like **"t"** in "tea"
- **ài** sounds like **"eye"** but with a sharp falling tone
- **tài** sounds like **"tie!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're emphasizing something excessive: **"tài!"** — that strong, emphatic tone is the
**fourth tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 太 (tài) - "too; excessively"
- 太好了 (tài hǎo le) - "that's great!"
- 太多 (tài duō) - "too many/much"
- 太太 (tài tai) - "Mrs.; wife"
- 太阳 (tài yáng) - "sun"
- 太晚 (tài wǎn) - "too late"

**💡 Special Notes:**

太 primarily functions as an adverb meaning "too" or "excessively," indicating that something
exceeds the normal or desired level. It's also used in formal titles (太太 for "Mrs.") and in the
word for "sun" (太阳). This character is essential for expressing emphasis and degree.
