A year; an annual cycle; a period of twelve months; age or time passage.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                   |
| -------------- | ---------------------- |
| Pinyin         | nián                   |
| Core meaning   | year; annual; age      |
| Part of speech | noun, measure word     |
| Tone           | second tone (rising)   |

## Visual Breakdown

年 represents **the annual harvest cycle and time's passage**.

| Component     | Visual Description                                           |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **禾** (grain) | Rice/grain plant (禾) - represents the agricultural cycle    |
| **千** (thousand) | Many/abundance (千) - shows the accumulated harvest/time |

## Mnemonic

Think of 年 as **the annual grain harvest that marks time's passage**:

- The grain component (禾) shows the agricultural year from planting to harvest
- The abundance component (千) represents the accumulated grain or completed cycles
- Like counting years by the number of successful harvests
- Each full cycle from seed to harvest marks one year
- Shows how humans historically measured time by agricultural seasons

This creates the image: **time measured by the rhythm of agricultural abundance**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

年 represents **annual cycles, time periods, and age**. It's used:

1. **Calendar years**: 今年 (jīnnián) - "this year"
2. **Age**: 我二十年 (wǒ èrshí nián) - "I am twenty years old"
3. **Duration**: 三年 (sān nián) - "three years"
4. **Festivals**: 新年 (xīnnián) - "New Year"

## Examples

- **去年** (qùnián) - "last year"
- **明年** (míngnián) - "next year"
- **每年** (měi nián) - "every year"
- **年轻** (niánqīng) - "young" (literally "year light")
- **年龄** (niánlíng) - "age"
- **过年** (guò nián) - "celebrate New Year"

## Time Expressions

年 in temporal contexts:

- **年代** (niándài) - "era; decade"
- **年级** (niánjí) - "grade level" (in school)
- **年初** (niánchū) - "beginning of the year"
- **年底** (niándǐ) - "end of the year"
- **年中** (niánzhōng) - "middle of the year"

## Cultural Significance

年 is central to Chinese culture:

- **Spring Festival**: 春节 (chūnjié) also called 过年 (guò nián)
- **Zodiac cycles**: 十二年 (shí'èr nián) twelve-year animal cycle
- **Respect for age**: Years represent wisdom and experience
- **Agricultural heritage**: Connection to farming cycles and seasons
- **Family traditions**: Annual gatherings and celebrations

## Measure Word Usage

年 functions as a measure word for:

- **Age**: 五年 (wǔ nián) - "five years old"
- **Duration**: 工作了十年 (gōngzuòle shí nián) - "worked for ten years"
- **Historical periods**: 唐朝三百年 (Táng cháo sānbǎi nián) - "300 years of Tang Dynasty"

## Grammar Functions

- **Noun**: 这是什么年？ (zhè shì shénme nián?) - "What year is this?"
- **Measure word**: 三年的工作 (sān nián de gōngzuò) - "three years of work"
- **Time marker**: 年轻的时候 (niánqīng de shíhou) - "when (I was) young"

## Cultural Context

年 embodies Chinese concepts of:

- **Cyclical time**: Life follows natural patterns and cycles
- **Harvest abundance**: Success measured by accumulated prosperity
- **Generational wisdom**: Each year adds experience and understanding
- **Community celebration**: Annual festivals strengthen social bonds

The character reflects the deep connection between human time-keeping and agricultural prosperity that shaped Chinese civilization.
