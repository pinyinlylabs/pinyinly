Without doubt; surely; certainly; definitely.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                  |
| -------------- | --------------------- |
| Pinyin         | yī dìng               |
| Core meaning   | certainly; definitely |
| Part of speech | adverb                |
| Tone           | 1st + 4th tone        |

## Visual Breakdown

一定 combines **one + fixed/settled** to express absolute certainty.

| Component | Meaning              | Contribution to 一定                |
| --------- | -------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **一**    | one; unity; single   | Represents absoluteness, no doubt   |
| **定**    | fixed; settled; calm | Represents stability, determination |

## Mnemonic

Think of 一定 as **one fixed point**:

- 一 (one) represents singularity - there's only ONE possibility
- 定 (settled/fixed) shows this possibility is locked in place
- Like a compass needle that points to magnetic north - it's fixed, certain, definite
- When something is "one and settled," there's no room for doubt

## Usage Examples

- **我一定去** (wǒ yī dìng qù) - "I will definitely go"
- **一定要小心** (yī dìng yào xiǎo xīn) - "must be careful"
- **他一定知道** (tā yī dìng zhī dào) - "he certainly knows"
- **一定不行** (yī dìng bù xíng) - "definitely won't work"

## Grammar Notes

一定 functions as:

- **Adverb**: Modifies verbs to show certainty (一定会来 - "will definitely come")
- **Modal expression**: Shows obligation or necessity (一定要 - "must/have to")
- **Emphatic marker**: Strengthens assertions and promises
- **Future certainty**: Often used with 会 (huì) for future events

## Cultural Context

In Chinese communication, 一定 carries strong commitment:

- **Promise weight**: Using 一定 makes a statement more binding
- **Social expectation**: Creates accountability in relationships
- **Confidence marker**: Shows the speaker's strong belief or determination
