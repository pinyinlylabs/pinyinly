Used to address one or more people directly; you; yourself.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                        |
| -------------- | --------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | nǐ                          |
| Core meaning   | you; yourself               |
| Part of speech | pronoun                     |
| Tone           | third tone (falling-rising) |

## Visual Breakdown

你 combines **person + body** to represent addressing another person.

| Component | Visual Description                                             |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **亻**    | Person radical (人) - indicates this relates to people       |
| **尔**    | You/body (尔) - classical form meaning "you"                |

## Mnemonic

Think of 你 as **"the person I'm speaking to"** or **"recognizing another person"**:

- The person radical (亻) shows this is about human relationships
- The "you" component (尔) directly indicates the person being addressed
- Like pointing to someone and acknowledging them as an individual
- Shows respect and recognition of the other person's identity

## Usage Examples

- **你好** (nǐ hǎo) - "hello; how are you"
- **你是谁？** (nǐ shì shéi?) - "Who are you?"
- **你们** (nǐ men) - "you all; you (plural)"
- **你的** (nǐ de) - "your; yours"

## Cultural Context

你 is fundamental for addressing others in Chinese:
- Essential for greetings, questions, and conversations
- Used in both formal and informal situations (though 您 is more formal)
- The basis for understanding Chinese social interaction
- Critical for showing respect and acknowledgment of others
- Foundation for building relationships and communication
