**🗣️ Pronunciation of 睛 (jīng)**

- **Pinyin:** jīng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Ahhhh"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **īng** sounds like **"ing"** in "sing" but held steady and high
- **jīng** sounds like **"jing"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like stating clearly: **"jīng—"** — that's the clear tone pattern of **jīng**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 睛 (jīng) - "eyeball; pupil"
- 眼睛 (yǎn jīng) - "eyes" (most common usage)
- 火眼金睛 (huǒ yǎn jīn jīng) - "eyes of fire and gold" (sharp eyesight)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **"jing"** with a steady, clear tone — the **eyeball** is the clear, focused part of the
eye that sees everything distinctly!

**📝 Note:**

睛 is rarely used alone and is most commonly seen in the compound 眼睛 (yǎn jīng) meaning "eyes".
