**🗣️ Pronunciation of 舌 (shé)**

- **Pinyin:** shé
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question with interest

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "sheep"
- **é** sounds like **"eh"** in "meh", but with second tone → rises up
- **shé** sounds like **"sheh"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Start low and rise smoothly, like saying **"What?"** with curiosity: **"shé?"** — that's the rising
pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 舌 (shé) - "tongue"
- 舌头 (shé tou) - "tongue"
- 口舌 (kǒu shé) - "argument; dispute"
- 母舌 (mǔ shé) - "mother tongue"

**💡 Anatomical Note:**

舌 (shé) literally means "tongue" and is used in both anatomical contexts and metaphorically in
expressions about speech and language.
