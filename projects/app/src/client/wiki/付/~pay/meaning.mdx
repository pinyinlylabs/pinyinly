To give money in exchange for goods or services.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                       |
| -------------- | -------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | fù                         |
| Core meaning   | pay; give money; hand over |
| Part of speech | verb                       |
| Tone           | 4th tone                   |

## Visual Breakdown

付 shows **handing over something valuable with the hand**.

| Component | Meaning                 | Contribution to 付              |
| --------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **付**    | hand over; deliver; pay | Shows the act of giving payment |

## Character Analysis

### 付 (pay)

付 depicts **a person handing something over**:

- **亻** (person radical) represents the person making payment
- **寸** (inch/measure) shows measured or calculated amount
- Together: a person giving a measured amount in exchange
- Shows the deliberate act of transferring value

## Mnemonic

Think of 付 as **"a person reaching out to hand over the exact amount"**:

- Picture someone carefully counting money and handing it over
- Like reaching into your wallet to pay for something
- The character shows the physical act of giving payment
- Represents the fair exchange of money for goods or services

## Usage Examples

- **付钱** (fù qián) - "pay money"
- **付款** (fù kuǎn) - "make payment"
- **支付** (zhī fù) - "pay; payment"
- **付费** (fù fèi) - "pay fees"
- **付出** (fù chū) - "pay out; give; sacrifice"

## Grammar Patterns

付 typically:

- **With money**: 付一百元 - "pay 100 yuan"
- **For services**: 付医疗费 - "pay medical fees"
- **Payment methods**: 用现金付 - "pay with cash"
- **In compounds**: 付款方式 - "payment method"

## Cultural Context

付 reflects Chinese transaction culture:

- **Fair exchange**: 付 emphasizes the mutual benefit in transactions
- **Responsibility**: 付 implies obligation to pay what is owed
- **Modern payments**: 付 used for all forms including digital payments
- **Business relationships**: Proper 付 maintains trust and ongoing relationships
- **Social order**: 付 represents fulfilling one's part in social contracts
