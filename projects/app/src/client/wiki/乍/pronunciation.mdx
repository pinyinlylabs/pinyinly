**🗣️ Pronunciation of 乍 (zhà)**

- **Pinyin:** zhà
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Stop!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **zh** like **"j"** in "jelly" but with tongue tip curled back
- **à** sounds like **"ah"** in "father" with a sharp falling tone
- **zhà** sounds like **"jah!"** with a sharp drop and curled tongue

**🎯 Mastering the "zh" sound:**

The **zh** in Chinese is **retroflex** (tongue tip curled back):

1. **Start with "j"** like in "jelly"
2. **Curl your tongue tip back** toward the roof of your mouth
3. **Keep the sound deeper** in your throat
4. **Think of it as "j" but more "throaty"**

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is **decisive and sudden** — perfect for **"suddenly"**!

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 乍 (zhà) - "suddenly, at first"
- 乍看 (zhà kàn) - "at first glance"
- 乍听 (zhà tīng) - "at first hearing"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**"Suddenly!"** — the sharp fourth tone captures that abrupt, unexpected feeling!
