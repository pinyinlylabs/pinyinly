**🗣️ Pronunciation of 冖 (mì)**

- **Pinyin:** mì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "more"
- **ì** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but with a sharp falling tone
- **mì** sounds like **"mee!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is sharp and **falling**:

Say it like you're covering something securely — **"mì!"** — firm and decisive.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 冖 (mì) - "cover" (radical meaning)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

This radical looks like a cover or roof — imagine firmly placing a cover with a decisive **"mì!"**

**📌 Note:**

冖 is primarily used as a radical component (the "cover" radical) in other characters
like 写, 军, 冠, rather than as a standalone character in modern Chinese. It represents the concept
of covering or enclosing.
