**🗣️ Pronunciation of 应 (yīng)**

- **Pinyin:** yīng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **īng** sounds like **"eeng"** held steady and high
- **yīng** sounds like **"yeeng"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Keep your voice high and steady, like singing a sustained note. **yīng** should sound confident and
even.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 应该 (yīng gāi) - "should; ought to"
- 应当 (yīng dāng) - "should; must"
- 反应 (fǎn yìng) - "reaction; response"
- 回应 (huí yìng) - "respond; reply"

**🔧 Special Notes:**

应 can also be pronounced **yìng** (fourth tone) in some contexts, especially when meaning "to
respond" or "to comply":

- 应付 (yìng fu) - "cope with"
- 适应 (shì yìng) - "adapt to"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

应 meaning "should" uses the **steady, reliable** first tone — just like how you **should** be
steady and reliable!
