**🗣️ Pronunciation of 指 (zhǐ)**

- **Pinyin:** zhǐ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **zh** like **"j"** in "jeer" but with tongue curled back
- **ǐ** sounds like **"ee"** in "see", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **zhǐ** sounds like **"jee"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking or being thoughtful: **"zhǐ..."** — that's the tone pattern of **zhǐ**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 指 (zhǐ) - "finger; to point; to indicate"
- 手指 (shǒu zhǐ) - "finger"
- 指出 (zhǐ chū) - "to point out"
- 指导 (zhǐ dǎo) - "to guide; guidance"
- 指向 (zhǐ xiàng) - "to point towards"

**💡 Pronunciation Note:**

The **zh** sound is a retroflex consonant - curl your tongue tip back towards the roof of your mouth
when making the sound.
