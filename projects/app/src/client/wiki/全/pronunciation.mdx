**🗣️ Pronunciation of 全 (quán)**

- **Pinyin:** quán
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **qu** like **"chw"** sound (similar to "qu" in "question")
- **án** sounds like **"ahn"** with a rising tone
- **quán** sounds like **"chwahn?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking a question: **"quán?"** — that's the tone pattern of **quán**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 全 (quán) - "all; complete"
- 全部 (quán bù) - "all; entire"
- 全国 (quán guó) - "nationwide; whole country"
- 全家 (quán jiā) - "whole family"
- 完全 (wán quán) - "completely; entirely"
- 全面 (quán miàn) - "comprehensive; all-around"

**💡 Special Notes:**

全 consistently uses the second tone (quán) and means "complete" or "all." It's often used to
emphasize completeness or totality in compound words.
