To do; to make; to act as; to be; to perform an action or task.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                  |
| -------------- | --------------------- |
| Pinyin         | zuò                   |
| Core meaning   | do; make; act as      |
| Part of speech | verb                  |
| Tone           | fourth tone (falling) |

## Visual Breakdown

做 combines **person + ancient** to represent human action and established practices.

| Component | Visual Description                                         |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **亻**    | Person radical (亻) - indicates human activity            |
| **古**    | Ancient/old (古) - represents established ways of doing   |

## Mnemonic

Think of 做 as **a person following ancient, established ways of doing things**:

- The person radical (亻) shows this involves human action
- The ancient component (古) represents traditional methods and established practices
- Like learning to do things the way they've been done for generations
- Shows human activity guided by traditional knowledge and methods
- Combines human effort with time-tested approaches

This creates the image: **people performing actions according to established methods**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

做 represents **performing actions, creating, and functioning as something**. It's used:

1. **Basic doing**: 做事 (zuò shì) - "do things; work"
2. **Making/creating**: 做饭 (zuò fàn) - "cook; make a meal"
3. **Acting as**: 做老师 (zuò lǎoshī) - "work as a teacher"
4. **Becoming**: 做朋友 (zuò péngyǒu) - "become friends"

## Examples

- **做工作** (zuò gōngzuò) - "do work; work"
- **做梦** (zuò mèng) - "dream; have a dream"
- **做人** (zuò rén) - "behave; conduct oneself"
- **做生意** (zuò shēngyì) - "do business"
- **做决定** (zuò juédìng) - "make a decision"
- **做运动** (zuò yùndòng) - "exercise; do sports"

- **做家具** (zuò jiājù) - "make furniture"

做 indicating roles or functions:

- **做医生** (zuò yīshēng) - "work as a doctor"
- **做学生** (zuò xuéshēng) - "be a student"
- **做父母** (zuò fùmǔ) - "be parents"
- **做客人** (zuò kèrén) - "be a guest"

## Behavioral Actions

做 describing conduct:

- **做好事** (zuò hǎo shì) - "do good deeds"
- **做坏事** (zuò huài shì) - "do bad things"
- **做榜样** (zuò bǎngyàng) - "set an example"
- **做准备** (zuò zhǔnbèi) - "make preparations"

## Work and Tasks

做 in professional contexts:

- **做项目** (zuò xiàngmù) - "work on a project"
- **做实验** (zuò shíyàn) - "do experiments"
- **做研究** (zuò yánjiū) - "conduct research"
- **做报告** (zuò bàogào) - "give a report"

## Common Expressions

- **做人做事** (zuò rén zuò shì) - "how to conduct oneself and handle affairs"
- **做贼心虚** (zuò zéi xīn xū) - "have a guilty conscience"
- **重新做人** (chóngxīn zuò rén) - "turn over a new leaf"
- **做一天和尚撞一天钟** - "work half-heartedly"

## Emotional and Abstract

做 with feelings and states:

- **做梦** (zuò mèng) - "dream; have dreams"
- **做鬼** (zuò guǐ) - "act like a ghost; sneak around"
- **做作** (zuòzuò) - "affected; artificial"
- **做主** (zuò zhǔ) - "make decisions; be in charge"

## Grammar Functions

- **Transitive verb**: 做什么？ (zuò shénme?) - "what to do?"
- **With objects**: 做功课 (zuò gōngkè) - "do homework"
- **As complement**: 当老师做 (dāng lǎoshī zuò) - "work as a teacher"
- **Progressive**: 在做 (zài zuò) - "in the process of doing"

## 做 vs. Similar Characters

Important distinctions:

- **做** (zuò): General doing, making, acting as
- **作** (zuò): Writing, creating artistic works, composition
- **坐** (zuò): Sitting, taking transportation

## Cultural Context

做 reflects Chinese values:

- **实践智慧** (shíjiàn zhìhuì) - Wisdom comes through doing
- **勤劳美德** (qínláo měidé) - Virtue of hard work and diligence
- **技能传承** (jìnéng chuánchéng) - Skills passed down through practice
- **人生态度** (rénshēng tàidù) - Attitude toward life and work

## Philosophy of Action

做 in Chinese thought:

- **知行合一** (zhī xíng héyī) - Unity of knowledge and action
- **实事求是** (shí shì qiú shì) - Seek truth from facts through doing
- **学而时习之** - Learning through repeated practice
- **做中学** (zuò zhōng xué) - Learning by doing

The character represents the fundamental human capacity for purposeful action, creation, and the assumption of roles, emphasizing that doing is central to human existence and development.
