**🗣️ Pronunciation of 玩 (wán)**

- **Pinyin:** wán
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking "wanna play?"

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "want"
- **án** sounds like **"ahn"** with rising tone
- **wán** sounds like **"wahn"** with playful rise

**🎯 Mastering the "án" final:**

The **án** combines:

1. **"a"** like "ah" in "father" — open and relaxed
2. **"n"** with tongue tip touching upper teeth/gums
3. **Rising tone** — starts low and lifts up cheerfully
4. **Forward "n"** — different from "ng", tongue stays forward

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) **rises** playfully:

Start **low and climb up** — like asking "wán?" (Want to play?) with enthusiasm

**📝 Practice words:**

- 玩 (wán) - "to play; to have fun"
- 玩儿 (wánr) - "to play" (northern dialect with 儿化)
- 玩具 (wán jù) - "toy"
- 好玩儿 (hǎo wánr) - "fun; interesting"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of a child asking to **play** — "wán?" with that excited, rising tone that shows eagerness and
hope. The rising tone captures the playful, inviting nature of the word.
