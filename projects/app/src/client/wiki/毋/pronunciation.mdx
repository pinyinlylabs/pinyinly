**🗣️ Pronunciation of 毋 (wú)**

- **Pinyin:** wú
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **ú** sounds like **"oo"** in "book" but with a **rising** tone
- **wú** sounds like **"woo?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ˊ)**

The **second tone** is **rising**, like asking a question:

Say it like you're asking "Who?": **"wú?"** — that questioning rise is the **second tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 毋 (wú) - "not to do; do not" (formal/classical)
- This character appears mainly in classical Chinese and formal expressions
- Similar to 无 (wú) meaning "without; not have"
- Found in classical phrases and formal writing

**💡 Special Notes:**

毋 is primarily used in classical Chinese and formal written contexts. It's a prohibition particle
meaning "do not" or "must not." In modern spoken Chinese, 不要 (bù yào) or 别 (bié) are more
commonly used for "don't" or "do not."
