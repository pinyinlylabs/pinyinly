The natural movement of air outdoors; wind; breeze; air current.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                      |
| -------------- | ------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | fēng                      |
| Core meaning   | wind; breeze; air current |
| Part of speech | noun                      |
| Tone           | first tone (high flat)    |

## Visual Breakdown

风 represents **wind moving through the landscape** in stylized form.

| Component | Visual Description                                        |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **风**    | Originally showed air currents moving through trees/sails |

The character evolved from depicting the visual effects of wind - movement, swaying, and dynamic
flow through the environment.

## Mnemonic

Think of 风 as **"invisible force creating visible movement everywhere"**:

- Picture leaves rustling, branches swaying, flags fluttering
- The invisible wind made visible through its effects on objects
- Like nature's breath moving through the world
- Air currents that you feel but cannot see
- The dynamic force that brings movement and change

This creates the image: **invisible air currents creating movement and change throughout the natural
world**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

风 represents **wind, air movement, and atmospheric flow**. It's used:

1. **Natural wind**: 刮风 (guā fēng) - "it's windy"
2. **Gentle breeze**: 微风 (wēi fēng) - "gentle breeze"
3. **Air current**: 风向 (fēng xiàng) - "wind direction"
4. **Style/manner**: 风格 (fēng gé) - "style; manner"

## Examples

- **大风** (dà fēng) - "strong wind"
- **小风** (xiǎo fēng) - "light wind; gentle breeze"
- **北风** (běi fēng) - "north wind"
- **南风** (nán fēng) - "south wind"
- **风雨** (fēng yǔ) - "wind and rain; storm"

## Wind Intensity

Different levels of 风:

- **微风** (wēi fēng) - "gentle breeze"
- **轻风** (qīng fēng) - "light wind"
- **大风** (dà fēng) - "strong wind"
- **狂风** (kuáng fēng) - "fierce wind; gale"

## Directional Winds

风 from different directions:

- **东风** (dōng fēng) - "east wind"
- **西风** (xī fēng) - "west wind"
- **南风** (nán fēng) - "south wind"
- **北风** (běi fēng) - "north wind"

## Seasonal Winds

风 through the year:

- **春风** (chūn fēng) - "spring breeze" (gentle, warming)
- **夏风** (xià fēng) - "summer wind" (cooling relief)
- **秋风** (qiū fēng) - "autumn wind" (crisp, cool)
- **冬风** (dōng fēng) - "winter wind" (cold, harsh)

## Cultural Significance

风 in Chinese culture represents:

**Natural Elements:**

- **风水** (fēngshuǐ) - "feng shui" (wind and water; geomancy)
- **风调雨顺** (fēng tiáo yǔ shùn) - "favorable weather" (wind and rain in harmony)
- **乘风破浪** (chéng fēng pò làng) - "ride the wind and break the waves" (overcome difficulties)

**Philosophical Concepts:**

- **变化之力** (biànhuà zhī lì) - Force of change and transformation
- **无形影响** (wúxíng yǐngxiǎng) - Invisible but powerful influence
- **自然节律** (zìrán jiélǜ) - Natural rhythms and cycles
- **流动能量** (liúdòng néngliàng) - Flowing energy and movement

## Metaphorical Uses

风 in figurative language:

- **风格** (fēng gé) - "style; manner"

- **风度** (fēng dù) - "bearing; demeanor"
- **风气** (fēng qì) - "general mood; atmosphere"

## Weather Phenomena

## Common Expressions

- **风雨同舟** (fēng yǔ tóng zhōu) - "weather the storm together"
- **顺风顺水** (shùn fēng shùn shuǐ) - "favorable wind and water; smooth sailing"
- **风和日丽** (fēng hé rì lì) - "gentle breeze and beautiful sun"
- **风起云涌** (fēng qǐ yún yǒng) - "wind rises and clouds surge" (turbulent times)

## Learning Notes

风 is essential because:

- Fundamental natural force affecting weather, environment, and daily life
- Essential for understanding Chinese cultural concepts (feng shui, expressions)
- Key to weather vocabulary and environmental discussions
- Important for metaphorical and philosophical language use
- Demonstrates how characters capture invisible natural forces

风 reflects the Chinese understanding that wind is both a physical force and a metaphor for change,
influence, and the invisible powers that shape our world!
