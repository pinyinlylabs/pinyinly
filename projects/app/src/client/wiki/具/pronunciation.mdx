**🗣️ Pronunciation of 具 (jù)**

- **Pinyin:** jù
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep" (but softer, more like "dz" sound)
- **ù** sounds like **"oo"** in "book" but with a sharp falling tone
- **jù** sounds like **"joo!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're giving a firm command: **"jù!"** — that's the tone pattern of **jù**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 具 (jù) - "tool; to have"
- 具体 (jù tǐ) - "specific; concrete"
- 具有 (jù yǒu) - "to have; to possess"
- 工具 (gōng jù) - "tool; instrument"
- 家具 (jiā jù) - "furniture"
- 玩具 (wán jù) - "toy"

**💡 Special Notes:**

具 consistently uses the fourth tone (jù) and can mean "tool" when used alone, or "to have/possess"
in compounds like 具有. It appears in many compound words related to tools, instruments, and
objects.
