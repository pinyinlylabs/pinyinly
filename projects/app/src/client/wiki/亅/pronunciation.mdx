**🗣️ Pronunciation of 亅 (jué)**

- **Pinyin:** jué
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **ué** sounds like **"üeh"** but with a rising tone
- **jué** sounds like **"jweh?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ˊ)**

The **second tone** is a **rising** tone, like when you're surprised or asking a question:

Say it like you're asking **"What?"** or **"Really?"** — that's the energy of **jué**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 亅 (jué) - "hook" (radical component)

**💡 Special Notes:**

亅 is primarily used as a radical component in Chinese characters. It represents a **hook** shape
and is rarely used as a standalone character. You'll see it as part of other characters like 了, 予,
and 子.
