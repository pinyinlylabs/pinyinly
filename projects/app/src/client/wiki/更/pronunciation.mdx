**🗣️ Pronunciation of 更 (gèng)**

- **Pinyin:** gèng
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **g** like **"g"** in "go"
- **èng** sounds like **"ung"** in "hung", but with fourth tone → sharp fall down
- **gèng** sounds like **"gung!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Start high and drop down sharply, like making an emphatic statement. Perfect for expressing "even
more" emphasis!

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 更多 (gèng duō) - "more; even more"
- 更好 (gèng hǎo) - "better; even better"
- 更加 (gèng jiā) - "even more; all the more"
- 更新 (gèng xīn) - "update; renew"

**📚 Special Notes:**

更 can also be pronounced **gēng** (first tone) in some contexts, meaning "watch" or "night watch"
(classical usage), but **gèng** (fourth tone) meaning "more" is much more common in modern Chinese.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

更 means "more" - when you want to emphasize "even MORE," you naturally use that emphatic, falling
fourth tone: **"gèng!"**
