**🗣️ Pronunciation of 奶 (nǎi)**

- **Pinyin:** nǎi
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **n** like **"n"** in "no"
- **ǎi** sounds like **"eye"**, but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **nǎi** sounds like **"nye"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking about something tasty: **"nǎi..."** — that contemplative dip-and-rise is
the **third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 奶 (nǎi) - "milk"
- 牛奶 (niú nǎi) - "cow's milk"
- 奶奶 (nǎi nai) - "grandmother (paternal)"
- 奶茶 (nǎi chá) - "milk tea"
- 奶油 (nǎi yóu) - "cream, butter"
- 豆奶 (dòu nǎi) - "soy milk"

**💡 Special Notes:**

奶 primarily means "milk" but also appears in the word for paternal grandmother (奶奶). The
character combines the "woman" radical (女) with 乃, suggesting the connection between women and
nurturing/milk production.

**📍 Usage Context:**

奶 is essential for food and drink vocabulary, especially in modern contexts with various milk
products and milk-based beverages like bubble tea (珍珠奶茶).
