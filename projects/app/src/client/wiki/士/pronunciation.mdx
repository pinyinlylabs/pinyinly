**🗣️ Pronunciation of 士 (shì)**

- **Pinyin:** shì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Shir!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "she" (but with tongue curled back)
- **ì** sounds like **"ir"** in "sir" but with a sharp falling tone
- **shì** sounds like **"shir"** with a decisive drop

**🎯 Mastering the Chinese "sh" sound:**

The **sh** in Chinese has **retroflex tongue position**:

1. **Curl tongue back** — tip pointing toward roof of mouth
2. **Make "sh" sound** — air flows around curled tongue
3. **Deeper resonance** — sounds fuller than English "sh"
4. **Keep it smooth** — not harsh or whistly

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is **authoritative and falling**:

Start **high and drop decisively** — like announcing a title: **"shì!"** (scholar!)

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 士 (shì) - "scholar"
- 战士 (zhàn shì) - "soldier"
- 护士 (hù shì) - "nurse"
- 博士 (bó shì) - "doctor (PhD)"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of a **scholar** speaking with authority — the sharp falling fourth tone reflects the respect
and gravitas of the title **士**!
