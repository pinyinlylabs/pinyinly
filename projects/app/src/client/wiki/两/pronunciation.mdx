**🗣️ Pronunciation of 两 (liǎng)**

- **Pinyin:** liǎng
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **l** like **"l"** in "love"
- **iǎng** sounds like **"yahng"** with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **liǎng** sounds like **"lyahng"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking or being thoughtful: **"liǎng..."** — that's the tone pattern of
**liǎng**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 两 (liǎng) - "two; pair; couple"
- 两个 (liǎng gè) - "two (of something)"
- 两天 (liǎng tiān) - "two days"
- 两边 (liǎng biān) - "both sides"
- 两年 (liǎng nián) - "two years"

**💡 Special Notes:**

两 specifically means "two" when used with measure words or in counting pairs. While 二 (èr) is the
basic number "two," 两 is used in most practical counting situations (两个人 "two people" rather
than 二个人). It emphasizes the concept of "a pair" or "both."
