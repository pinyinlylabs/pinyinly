**🗣️ Pronunciation of 善 (shàn)**

- **Pinyin:** shàn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Good!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "sheep" but with tongue curled back
- **àn** sounds like **"ahn"** in "Khan" but with a sharp falling tone
- **shàn** sounds like **"shahn"** with a firm, decisive drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're making a firm statement: **"shàn!"** — that's the tone pattern of **shàn**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 善 (shàn) - "good; kind; virtuous"
- 善良 (shàn liáng) - "kind-hearted"
- 善意 (shàn yì) - "good intention"
- 改善 (gǎi shàn) - "to improve"
- 慈善 (cí shàn) - "charity"

**💡 Special Notes:**

善 represents moral goodness and virtue, often used in philosophical and ethical contexts. It's more
formal than 好 (hǎo) and emphasizes kindness, benevolence, and moral excellence. The character is
fundamental in Chinese philosophy and literature when discussing virtue and righteousness.
