**🗣️ Pronunciation of 冫 (bīng)**

- **Pinyin:** bīng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Eeee"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **b** like **"b"** in "bee"
- **īng** sounds like **"eeng"** but held steady and high
- **bīng** sounds like **"beeng"** with steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is **high and flat**:

Keep your voice **steady and high** throughout — like the sound of ice crystals: **"bīīīng"**

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 冫 (bīng) - "ice" (radical meaning)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

This radical looks like two drops of ice — imagine the clear, steady sound of ice: **"bīng"**!

**📌 Note:**

冫 is primarily used as a radical component (the "ice" radical) in other characters
like 冷 (cold), 冰 (ice), 冻 (freeze), rather than as a standalone character in modern Chinese. It
represents concepts related to cold or ice.
