**🗣️ Pronunciation of 日 (rì)**

- **Pinyin:** rì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **r** like **"r"** in "red" (but slightly rolled)
- **ì** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but with fourth tone → sharp falling tone
- **rì** sounds like **"ree!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a sharp **falling** tone:

Say it like you're pointing decisively at the sun: **"rì!"** — that sharp, definitive tone matches
the certainty of daylight!

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 日 (rì) - "day, sun"
- 今日 (jīn rì) - "today" (formal)
- 日子 (rì zi) - "days, life"
- 日期 (rì qī) - "date"
- 生日 (shēng rì) - "birthday"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

The sun is decisive and definite — just like the sharp fourth tone of **rì**!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

日 originally depicted the sun and is one of the most fundamental characters in Chinese. It appears
in many compound words related to time and dates, and is also the character for Japan (日本).
