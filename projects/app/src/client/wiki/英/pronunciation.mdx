**🗣️ Pronunciation of 英 (yīng)**

- **Pinyin:** yīng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **īng** sounds like **"ing"** but with first tone → steady and high
- **yīng** sounds like **"ying"** held steady and high

**🎧 Tone tip (First tone: ˉ)**

The **first tone** is **high and flat**, like singing a steady note:

Say it like you're proudly naming Britain: **"yīng"** — that steady, high pitch is the **first
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 英 (yīng) - "Britain, English"
- 英国 (yīng guó) - "United Kingdom, Britain"
- 英语 (yīng yǔ) - "English language"
- 英文 (yīng wén) - "English (written)"
- 英雄 (yīng xióng) - "hero"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **英** as confidently declaring "English!" — that steady, clear pronunciation matches the
**first tone**!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

英 is commonly used to refer to Britain and the English language, and also appears in positive terms
like 英雄 (hero), connecting to ideas of excellence and distinction.
