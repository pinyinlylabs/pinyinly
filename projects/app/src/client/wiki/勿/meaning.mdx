## Core Meaning & Usage

勿 represents **prohibition, warning against actions, and moral imperatives to avoid something**.
It's used:

1. **Classical prohibition**: 勿忘 (wù wàng) - "do not forget"
2. **Moral guidance**: 己所不欲，勿施于人 - "don't do to others what you don't want done to you"
3. **Formal warnings**: 勿扰 (wù rǎo) - "do not disturb"
4. **Instructions**: 勿碰 (wù pèng) - "do not touch"

## Examples

- **勿忘** (wù wàng) - "do not forget"
- **勿扰** (wù rǎo) - "do not disturb"
- **勿碰** (wù pèng) - "do not touch"
- **切勿** (qiè wù) - "must not; never"
- **勿用** (wù yòng) - "do not use"

## Cultural Context

勿 appears in classical Chinese literature and Confucian teachings, often in moral contexts. The
famous saying "己所不欲，勿施于人" (don't do to others what you don't want done to you) uses 勿 to
express the golden rule. In modern Chinese, 勿 is used in formal signs and instructions, carrying a
sense of serious prohibition or moral imperative.
