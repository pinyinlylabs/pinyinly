Male; man; masculine; boy; son; male gender.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                 |
| -------------- | -------------------- |
| Pinyin         | nán                  |
| Core meaning   | male; man; masculine |
| Part of speech | noun, adjective      |
| Tone           | second tone (rising) |

## Visual Breakdown

男 combines **field + strength** to represent masculine roles and power.

| Component | Visual Description                                        |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **田**    | Field (田) - represents agricultural work and cultivation |
| **力**    | Strength/power (力) - shows physical force and energy     |

## Mnemonic

Think of 男 as **using strength to work the fields**:

- The field component (田) represents agricultural labor and cultivation
- The strength component (力) shows physical power and energy
- Like men traditionally working in the fields using physical strength
- Shows the combination of land cultivation with masculine power
- Represents the traditional role of men in agricultural societies

This creates the image: **masculine strength applied to cultivating the land**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

男 represents **male gender and masculinity**. It's used:

1. **Gender**: 男人 (nán rén) - "man; male person"
2. **Family**: 男孩 (nán hái) - "boy"
3. **Relationships**: 男朋友 (nán péngyǒu) - "boyfriend"
4. **Description**: 男性 (nán xìng) - "male; masculine"

## Examples

- **男子** (nán zǐ) - "man; male"
- **男士** (nán shì) - "gentleman; Mr."
- **男生** (nán shēng) - "male student; boy"
- **男儿** (nán ér) - "man; male (literary)"
- **男女** (nán nǚ) - "male and female; men and women"
- **男装** (nán zhuāng) - "men's clothing"

## Traditional Roles

男 in cultural contexts:

- **男主角** (nán zhǔjué) - "male protagonist"
- **男子汉** (nán zǐhàn) - "real man; masculine man"
- **男尊女卑** (nán zūn nǚ bēi) - "male superiority" (traditional concept)
- **男耕女织** (nán gēng nǚ zhī) - "men plow, women weave"

## Modern Usage

男 in contemporary contexts:

- **男神** (nán shén) - "male idol; dream guy"
- **男团** (nán tuán) - "boy band"
- **男科** (nán kē) - "men's medicine; andrology"
- **男性化** (nán xìng huà) - "masculinization"

## Cultural Context

男 reflects evolving concepts of masculinity:

- **Traditional**: Physical strength, provider role, family leadership
- **Modern**: Diverse expressions of masculinity, emotional intelligence
- **Contemporary**: Gender equality, shared responsibilities
- **Balance**: Integration with feminine qualities (阴阳 balance)

The character represents the social and biological aspects of male identity while reflecting
changing cultural understanding of gender roles.
