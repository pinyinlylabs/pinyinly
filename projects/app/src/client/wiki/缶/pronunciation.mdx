**🗣️ Pronunciation of 缶 (fǒu)**

- **Pinyin:** fǒu
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **f** like **"f"** in "free"
- **ǒu** sounds like **"oh"** in "oh no", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **fǒu** sounds like **"foh"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking about ancient pottery: **"fǒu..."** — that thoughtful dip and rise.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 缶 (fǒu) - "earthenware jar; pottery vessel"
- 否 (fǒu) - "no; not" (different character, same sound)
- 瓦缶 (wǎ fǒu) - "earthenware pot"

**📚 Cultural Note:**

缶 (fǒu) is an ancient Chinese character representing earthenware vessels used for storage. It's
also used as a radical in other characters related to containers.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Ancient **earthenware** was made thoughtfully and carefully — that contemplative process matches the
third tone!
