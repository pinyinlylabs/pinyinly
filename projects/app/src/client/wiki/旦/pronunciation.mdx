**🗣️ Pronunciation of 旦 (dàn)**

- **Pinyin:** dàn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "dog"
- **àn** sounds like **"ahn"** in "barn" but with fourth tone → sharp falling tone
- **dàn** sounds like **"dahn!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a sharp **falling** tone:

Say it like you're announcing the break of dawn: **"dàn!"** — that decisive tone captures the
definitive moment when day begins!

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 旦 (dàn) - "dawn, morning, day"
- 元旦 (yuán dàn) - "New Year's Day"
- 一旦 (yī dàn) - "once, as soon as"
- 旦夕 (dàn xī) - "morning and evening, short time"
- 旦日 (dàn rì) - "dawn, daybreak" (literary)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Dawn** breaks decisively and suddenly — just like the sharp fourth tone of **dàn**!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

旦 combines the sun (日) with a horizontal line representing the horizon, visually depicting the sun
rising at dawn. It's commonly seen in 元旦 (New Year's Day).
