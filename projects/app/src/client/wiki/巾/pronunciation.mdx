**🗣️ Pronunciation of 巾 (jīn)**

- **Pinyin:** jīn
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Jeen"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep" but **lighter** and **more forward** in the mouth
- **īn** sounds like **"een"** in "seen" with first tone → high and steady
- **jīn** sounds like **"jeen"** with a high, flat pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're naming a piece of cloth steadily: **"jīn..."** — that's the steady tone of
**jīn**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 巾 (jīn) - "cloth; towel"
- 毛巾 (máo jīn) - "towel"
- 手巾 (shǒu jīn) - "handkerchief"
- 围巾 (wéi jīn) - "scarf"
- 纸巾 (zhǐ jīn) - "tissue"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **巾** as looking like a hanging towel — that steady **first tone** shows the reliable,
practical nature of cloth!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

巾 is also a radical (布巾旁) found in many characters related to cloth and fabric.
