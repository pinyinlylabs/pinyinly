**🗣️ Pronunciation of 氐 (dī)**

- **Pinyin:** dī
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Dee"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "deep"
- **ī** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but held steady and high
- **dī** sounds like **"dee"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip (First tone: ˉ)**

The **first tone** is **high and flat**, like holding a steady note:

Say it like you're pointing to the bottom of something: **"dī..."** — that steady, high tone is the
**first tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 氐 (dī) - "bottom; base; foundation"
- Used mainly as a radical component in other characters
- Related to concepts of foundation or bottom
- Appears in characters like:
  - 低 (dī) - "low"
  - 底 (dǐ) - "bottom"

**💡 Special Notes:**

氐 is primarily used as a radical component in modern Chinese rather than as a standalone word. It
conveys the meaning of "bottom" or "foundation" and appears in characters related to low positions
or bases.
