**🗣️ Pronunciation of 殳 (shū)**

- **Pinyin:** shū
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Shoo"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "shush"
- **ū** sounds like **"oo"** in "moon" but held steady and high
- **shū** sounds like **"shoo"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip (First tone: ˉ)**

The **first tone** is **high and flat**, like holding a steady note:

Say it like you're quietly shooing something away: **"shū..."** — that steady, high tone is the
**first tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 殳 (shū) - "weapon; ancient weapon with wooden handle"
- Used as a radical in characters like:
  - 段 (duàn) - "paragraph; section"
  - 毁 (huǐ) - "destroy"

**💡 Special Notes:**

殳 is primarily used as a radical component in other characters rather than as a standalone word in
modern Chinese. It originally referred to an ancient weapon with a wooden handle and was used for
striking. In modern usage, you'll mainly encounter it as part of compound characters.
