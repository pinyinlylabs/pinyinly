**🗣️ Pronunciation of 头 (tóu)**

- **Pinyin:** tóu
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **t** like **"t"** in "top"
- **óu** sounds like **"oh"**, but with second tone → rising pitch
- **tóu** sounds like **"toe?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking "Really?": **"tóu?"** — that's the upward rise of the **second tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 头 (tóu) - "head"
- 头发 (tóu fà) - "hair"
- 头痛 (tóu tòng) - "headache"
- 石头 (shí tóu) - "stone"
- 馒头 (mán tóu) - "steamed bread"
- 从头开始 (cóng tóu kāi shǐ) - "start from the beginning"

**💡 Special Notes:**

头 is a very common and versatile character in Chinese. It can mean "head" literally, but also
appears in many compound words where it can mean "beginning," "top," or serve as a suffix for
various objects (like 石头 for "stone").

**📍 Usage Patterns:**

- As a body part: 头 (head)
- As a suffix: 石头 (stone), 木头 (wood)
- In expressions: 头一次 (first time), 头等 (first class)
