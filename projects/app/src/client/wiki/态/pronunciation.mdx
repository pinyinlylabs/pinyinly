**🗣️ Pronunciation of 态 (tài)**

- **Pinyin:** tài
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **t** like **"t"** in "top" but with a puff of air
- **ài** sounds like **"I"** in "I am" but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **tài** sounds like **"tie!"** with a sharp falling tone

**🎯 Mastering the "t" sound:**

The **t** in Chinese is **aspirated** (has a puff of air):

1. **Start with "t"** like in "top"
2. **Add a strong puff of air** — hold your hand in front of your mouth and feel the air
3. **Make it breathy** — like blowing out a small candle
4. **Think "t" + breath**

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a sharp **falling** tone:

Say it like you're making a definitive statement: **"tài!"** — that's the tone pattern of **tài**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 态 (tài) - "manner, attitude"
- 态度 (tài du) - "attitude"
- 状态 (zhuàng tài) - "state, condition"
- 形态 (xíng tài) - "form, shape"
- 生态 (shēng tài) - "ecology"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Manner** with the sharp fourth tone — like firmly declaring your attitude!
