**🗣️ Pronunciation of 贝 (bèi)**

- **Pinyin:** bèi
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — **falling** tone, like stating a fact: **"That's it!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **b** like **"b"** in "bay"
- **èi** sounds like **"ay"** in "bay" but with a sharp falling tone
- **bèi** sounds like **"bay!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip (Fourth tone: ˋ)**

The **fourth tone** is **sharp and falling**, like making a definitive statement:

Say it like you're pointing out a shell: **"bèi!"** — that decisive, downward tone is the **fourth
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 贝 (bèi) - "shell; shellfish"
- 贝壳 (bèi ké) - "shell; seashell"
- 扇贝 (shàn bèi) - "scallop"
- 海贝 (hǎi bèi) - "sea shell"

**💡 Radical Significance:**

贝 (bèi) is also the **"shell radical"** (贝部) and appears in many characters related to money,
treasure, and value:

- 钱 (qián) - "money"
- 财 (cái) - "wealth"
- 买 (mǎi) - "buy"
- 卖 (mài) - "sell"
- 贵 (guì) - "expensive"

**🏛️ Historical Note:**

This connection comes from ancient China, where shells (especially cowrie shells) were used as
currency. The shell radical in money-related characters reflects this historical use of shells as a
medium of exchange.
