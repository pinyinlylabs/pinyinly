**🗣️ Pronunciation of 夊 (suī)**

- **Pinyin:** suī
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Swee"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **s** like **"s"** in "see"
- **uī** sounds like **"way"** but held steady and high
- **suī** sounds like **"sway"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're steadily describing slow movement: **"suī..."** — that's the tone pattern of
**suī**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 夊 (suī) - "go slowly" (radical form)
- 夊部 (suī bù) - "go slowly radical section"

**💡 Special Notes:**

夊 is primarily used as a radical in Chinese characters. It represents the concept of slow movement
or walking slowly. This character is rarely used independently in modern Chinese but appears as a
component in other characters like 处 (chù) and 复 (fù).
