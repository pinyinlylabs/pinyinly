**🗣️ Pronunciation of 声 (shēng)**

- **Pinyin:** shēng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Sheng"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "she" (but with tongue curled back)
- **ēng** sounds like **"ung"** but held steady and high
- **shēng** sounds like **"shung"** with a steady high pitch

**🎯 Mastering the Chinese "sh" sound:**

The **sh** in Chinese has **retroflex tongue position**:

1. **Curl tongue back** — tip pointing toward roof of mouth
2. **Make "sh" sound** — air flows around curled tongue
3. **Deeper resonance** — sounds fuller than English "sh"
4. **Keep it smooth** — not harsh or whistly

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like holding a steady, high musical note: **"shēng"** — maintain that high pitch throughout,
just like a continuous **sound**!

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 声 (shēng) - "sound"
- 声音 (shēng yīn) - "sound"
- 声明 (shēng míng) - "statement"
- 大声 (dà shēng) - "loudly"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **声** as a steady **sound** wave — the high, flat first tone represents a consistent audio
frequency!
