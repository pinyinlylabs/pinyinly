**🗣️ Pronunciation of 惯 (guàn)**

- **Pinyin:** guàn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like stating a firm habit

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **gu** like **"goo"** in "goose"
- **àn** sounds like **"ahn"** with a sharp falling tone and nasal ending
- **guàn** sounds like **"gwahn"** with a decisive downward drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're firmly stating a habit: **"guàn!"** — that decisive drop captures the certainty
of an established pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 惯 (guàn) - "habit; accustomed to"
- 习惯 (xí guàn) - "habit; custom; to be used to"
- 惯例 (guàn lì) - "convention; usual practice"
- 看惯 (kàn guàn) - "to be used to seeing"
- 听惯 (tīng guàn) - "to be used to hearing"

**🎭 Usage Note:**

惯 (guàn) often appears in compound words expressing being accustomed to something or having
developed a habit. It carries the sense of something that has become natural through repetition.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of how you firmly say "I'm used to it!" — **"guàn!"** — that definitive, downward tone
reflects the settled nature of a habit!
