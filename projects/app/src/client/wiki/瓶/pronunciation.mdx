**🗣️ Pronunciation of 瓶 (píng)**

- **Pinyin:** píng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking about a bottle

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **p** like **"p"** in "pat" with strong puff of air
- **íng** sounds like **"eeng"** with rising tone
- **píng** sounds like **"peeng"** with questioning rise

**🎯 Mastering the "p" sound:**

The **p** in Chinese is:

1. **Strongly aspirated** — big puff of air follows
2. **Lips together** — then explosive release
3. **Test:** hold paper in front of mouth — it should flutter strongly
4. **More air than English** — emphasize the breath

**🎯 Mastering the "íng" final:**

The **íng** combines:

1. **"i"** like "ee" in "see" but brief
2. **"ng"** like at the end of "sing" — tongue back touching soft palate
3. **Rising tone** — starts low and climbs up
4. **Nasal resonance** — let it echo in your nose and throat

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) **rises** curiously:

Start **low and lift up** — like asking "píng?" (Is that a bottle?)

**📝 Practice words:**

- 瓶 (píng) - "bottle; vase"
- 瓶子 (píng zi) - "bottle"
- 花瓶 (huā píng) - "flower vase"
- 水瓶 (shuǐ píng) - "water bottle"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of picking up an interesting **bottle** — "píng?" with that rising, curious tone as you wonder
what might be inside or what kind of bottle it is.
