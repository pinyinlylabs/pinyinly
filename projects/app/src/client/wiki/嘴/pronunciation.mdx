**🗣️ Pronunciation of 嘴 (zuǐ)**

- **Pinyin:** zuǐ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **z** like **"ds"** in "ads" but sharper and more precise
- **uǐ** sounds like **"way"** but with the **third tone** → dip down and rise up
- **zuǐ** sounds like **"dzway"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're pointing to your mouth thoughtfully: **"zuǐ..."** — that's the tone pattern of
**zuǐ**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 嘴 (zuǐ) - "mouth"
- 嘴巴 (zuǐ ba) - "mouth" (more colloquial)
- 张嘴 (zhāng zuǐ) - "to open one's mouth"
- 闭嘴 (bì zuǐ) - "shut up; be quiet"
- 嘴唇 (zuǐ chún) - "lips"

**💡 Special Notes:**

嘴 is the common word for "mouth" in everyday conversation. It's more casual than the
formal 口 (kǒu). The character is essential for describing facial features, eating, speaking, and
expressions involving the mouth.
