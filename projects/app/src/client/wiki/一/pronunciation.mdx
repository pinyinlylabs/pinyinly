**🗣️ Pronunciation of 一**

一 has **tone change rules** that affect how it's pronounced:

**📍 yī (first tone) - default pronunciation**

- **Pinyin:** yī
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Eeee"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **ī** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but held steady and high
- **yī** sounds like **"yee"** with a steady high pitch

**📍 yí (second tone) - before fourth tone**

- **Pinyin:** yí
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **í** sounds like **"ee"** but with a rising tone
- **yí** sounds like **"yee?"** with a questioning rise

**📍 yì (fourth tone) - before first, second, third tones**

- **Pinyin:** yì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **ì** sounds like **"ee"** but with a sharp falling tone
- **yì** sounds like **"yee!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone Change Rules:**

**一 changes to second tone (yí) before fourth tone:**

- 一定 → **yí** dìng - "definitely"
- 一样 → **yí** yàng - "same"
- 一半 → **yí** bàn - "half"

**一 changes to fourth tone (yì) before first, second, third tones:**

- 一天 → **yì** tiān - "one day" (first tone follows)
- 一年 → **yì** nián - "one year" (second tone follows)
- 一点 → **yì** diǎn - "a little" (third tone follows)

**一 stays first tone (yī) when alone or at end of words:**

- 一 (yī) - "one" (by itself)
- 十一 (shí yī) - "eleven" (at the end)
- 第一 (dì yī) - "first" (at the end)

**📝 Common Examples:**

**yī (first tone - standalone/final):**

- 一 (yī) - "one"
- 十一 (shí yī) - "eleven"
- 第一 (dì yī) - "first"

**yí (second tone before fourth tone):**

- 一定 (yí dìng) - "definitely"
- 一样 (yí yàng) - "same"
- 一会儿 (yí huì er) - "a while"

**yì (fourth tone before 1st/2nd/3rd tones):**

- 一天 (yì tiān) - "one day"
- 一年 (yì nián) - "one year"
- 一点 (yì diǎn) - "a little"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

一 is like a chameleon — it changes its tone to "fit in" with what comes after it, avoiding tone
clashes!
