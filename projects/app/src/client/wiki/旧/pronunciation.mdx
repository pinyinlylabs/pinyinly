**🗣️ Pronunciation of 旧 (jiù)**

- **Pinyin:** jiù
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **iù** sounds like **"yo"** in "yo-yo" but with fourth tone → sharp falling tone
- **jiù** sounds like **"jyo!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a sharp **falling** tone:

Say it like you're definitively stating something is old: **"jiù!"** — that decisive tone emphasizes
the certainty of age!

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 旧 (jiù) - "old, former, past"
- 旧的 (jiù de) - "old (thing)"
- 怀旧 (huái jiù) - "nostalgic, to feel nostalgic"
- 旧书 (jiù shū) - "old book, used book"
- 陈旧 (chén jiù) - "old-fashioned, obsolete"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

When something is definitively **old** (not new), you state it with certainty — just like the sharp
fourth tone of **jiù**!

**📍 Usage Note:**

旧 contrasts with 新 (new). It can refer to physical age (old objects) or temporal relationships
(former, ex-, past). The tone difference helps distinguish it from 九 (nine, third tone).
