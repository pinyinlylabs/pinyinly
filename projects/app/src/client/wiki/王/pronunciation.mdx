**🗣️ Pronunciation of 王 (wáng)**

- **Pinyin:** wáng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking "who's the king?"

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "want"
- **áng** sounds like **"ahng"** with rising tone, similar to "wrong" but rising
- **wáng** sounds like **"wahng"** with questioning rise

**🎯 Mastering the "áng" final:**

The **áng** combines:

1. **"a"** like "ah" in "father" — open and clear
2. **"ng"** like at the end of "sing" — tongue back touching soft palate
3. **Rising tone** — starts low and climbs up
4. **Nasal resonance** — let it echo in your nose and throat

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) **rises** like asking a question:

Start **low and climb up** — like asking "wáng?" (Is he the king?)

**📝 Practice words:**

- 王 (wáng) - "king; surname Wang"
- 王子 (wáng zi) - "prince"
- 王后 (wáng hòu) - "queen"
- 国王 (guó wáng) - "king of a country"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Imagine meeting royalty — you ask "wáng?" with that rising, respectful tone, wondering if this
person is indeed the **king**. The questioning rise shows your deference and curiosity.
