**🗣️ Pronunciation of 堂 (táng)**

- **Pinyin:** táng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Tahng?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **t** like **"t"** in "top"
- **áng** sounds like **"ahng"** with rising tone, similar to "wrong" but with Chinese nasal ending
- **táng** sounds like **"tahng"** with an upward inflection

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Start mid-level and rise up like asking **"táng?"** — like you're questioning "hall?"

**🎯 Mastering the "áng" final:**

The **áng** ending is **nasalized**:

1. **Start with "ah"** like in "father"
2. **Add "ng"** like at the end of "sing"
3. **Keep tongue back** — "ng" sound comes from back of tongue touching soft palate
4. **Make it resonate** — let the sound echo in your nose and throat

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 堂 (táng) - "hall"
- 课堂 (kè táng) - "classroom"
- 食堂 (shí táng) - "dining hall"
- 礼堂 (lǐ táng) - "auditorium"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of your voice **rising** as you enter a grand **hall** — the second tone echoes like sound
bouncing off high ceilings!
