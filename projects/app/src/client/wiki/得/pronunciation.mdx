**🗣️ Pronunciation of 得**

得 has **three different pronunciations** depending on its usage:

**📍 de (neutral tone) - complement particle**

- **Pinyin:** de (neutral tone)
- **Tone: Neutral tone** — **light and quick**, unstressed

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "day"
- **e** sounds like **"uh"** but very light and quick
- **de** sounds like a quick **"duh"**

**📍 dé (second tone) - "to get, to obtain"**

- **Pinyin:** dé
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "day"
- **é** sounds like **"ay"** in "day" but with a rising tone
- **dé** sounds like **"day?"** with a questioning rise

**📍 děi (third tone) - "must, have to"**

- **Pinyin:** děi
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh"

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "day"
- **ěi** sounds like **"ay"** but with the third tone → dip down and rise up
- **děi** sounds like **"day"** with a dip-then-rise

**📝 Usage Guide:**

**得 (de) - complement particle (most common):**

- 跑**得**很快 (pǎo de hěn kuài) - "runs very fast"
- 说**得**对 (shuō de duì) - "said correctly"
- 好**得**很 (hǎo de hěn) - "very good"

**得 (dé) - "to get, to obtain":**

- **得**到 (dé dào) - "to get, to obtain"
- **得**分 (dé fēn) - "to score points"
- 取**得** (qǔ dé) - "to achieve"

**得 (děi) - "must, have to":**

- 我**得**走了 (wǒ děi zǒu le) - "I have to go"
- **得**小心 (děi xiǎo xīn) - "must be careful"
- 这**得**花很多钱 (zhè děi huā hěn duō qián) - "this will cost a lot of money"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**de** = light grammatical connector (like -ly in "quickly") **dé** = "get it!" — obtaining
something successfully **děi** = "gotta" — expressing necessity with emphasis
