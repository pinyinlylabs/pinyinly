**🗣️ Pronunciation of 臣 (chén)**

- **Pinyin:** chén
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question with curiosity

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "cheese"
- **én** sounds like **"un"** in "fun", but with second tone → rises up
- **chén** sounds like **"chen"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Start low and rise up smoothly, like saying **"Really?"** when surprised: **"chén?"** — that's the
tone pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 臣 (chén) - "minister; court official"
- 大臣 (dà chén) - "minister; high official"
- 忠臣 (zhōng chén) - "loyal subject"

**💡 Cultural Note:**

臣 (chén) historically refers to court ministers or officials who serve the emperor. In modern
Chinese, it's mainly used in formal or historical contexts.
