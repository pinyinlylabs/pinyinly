**🗣️ Pronunciation of 曾 (céng)**

- **Pinyin:** céng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question with enthusiasm

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **c** like **"ts"** in "cats"
- **éng** sounds like **"ung"** in "hung", but with rising tone → goes up like asking a question
- **céng** sounds like **"tseng?"** with an upward rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Start low and rise up high, like asking "Really?" The rising tone fits the meaning of something that
happened in the past.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 曾经 (céng jīng) - "once; formerly; have ever"
- 曾经是 (céng jīng shì) - "used to be"
- 不曾 (bù céng) - "never; not ever"

**📚 Special Notes:**

曾 can also be pronounced **zēng** (first tone) when used as a surname, but **céng** (second tone)
meaning "already/once" is the primary pronunciation for the adverb usage.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

曾 means "already" or "once" - when recalling something from the past, you naturally use that
questioning, reminiscent tone: **"céng?"** (remember when...?)
