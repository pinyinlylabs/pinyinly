**🗣️ Pronunciation of 慢 (màn)**

- **Pinyin:** màn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like saying "slow down!"

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "man"
- **àn** sounds like **"ahn"** with a sharp falling tone and nasal ending
- **màn** sounds like **"mahn"** with a definitive downward drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're telling someone to slow down: **"màn!"** — that firm, commanding drop captures
the idea of reducing speed or taking your time.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 慢 (màn) - "slow; slowly"
- 慢慢 (màn màn) - "slowly; gradually"
- 很慢 (hěn màn) - "very slow"
- 慢走 (màn zǒu) - "walk slowly; take care" (polite farewell)
- 快慢 (kuài màn) - "fast and slow; speed"
- 慢性 (màn xìng) - "chronic; slow-developing"

**🎭 Usage Note:**

慢 (màn) describes tempo, pace, and speed. It can be used both as an adjective (slow) and as an
adverb (slowly), often appearing in contexts about time, movement, and patience.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of firmly telling someone to "slow down!" — **"màn!"** — that decisive falling tone perfectly
matches the commanding nature of asking someone to go slower!
