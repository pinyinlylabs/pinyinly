**🗣️ Pronunciation of 草 (cǎo)**

- **Pinyin:** cǎo
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **c** like **"ts"** in "cats"
- **ǎo** sounds like **"ow"** but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **cǎo** sounds like **"tsao"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is a **fall–then-rise** tone, like being thoughtful:

Say it like you're contemplating grass growing: **"cǎo..."** — that thoughtful dip-and-rise is the
**third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 草 (cǎo) - "grass"
- 草地 (cǎo dì) - "grassland, lawn"
- 小草 (xiǎo cǎo) - "small grass"
- 青草 (qīng cǎo) - "green grass"
- 草本 (cǎo běn) - "herbaceous"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **草** as grass swaying thoughtfully in the breeze — that up-and-down motion matches the
**third tone**!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

草 appears in many Chinese expressions and contains the grass radical 艹. It's used both literally
for grass and metaphorically in phrases about nature and growth.
