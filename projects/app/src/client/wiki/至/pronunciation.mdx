**🗣️ Pronunciation of 至 (zhì)**

- **Pinyin:** zhì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **zh** like **"j"** in "judge" (retroflex zh sound)
- **ì** sounds like **"er"** in "her", but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **zhì** sounds like **"jir"** with a decisive falling tone

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it firmly, like announcing arrival: **"zhì!"** — confident and definitive.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 至 (zhì) - "arrive; reach; until"
- 至今 (zhì jīn) - "until now; so far"
- 至少 (zhì shǎo) - "at least"
- 甚至 (shèn zhì) - "even; so much so that"
- 冬至 (dōng zhì) - "winter solstice"

**💡 Usage Note:**

至 (zhì) often appears in formal or written Chinese, commonly meaning "arrive at" or "reach" a point
in time or space.
