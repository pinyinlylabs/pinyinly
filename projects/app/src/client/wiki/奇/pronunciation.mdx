**🗣️ Pronunciation of 奇 (qí)**

- **Pinyin:** qí
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **q** like **"ch"** in "cheap" (but more back in the throat)
- **í** sounds like **"ee"** in "see", but with second tone → rising pitch
- **qí** sounds like **"chee?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're surprised and asking: **"qí?"** — that's the upward curiosity of the **second
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 奇 (qí) - "strange" (often in compounds)
- 奇怪 (qí guài) - "strange, odd"
- 好奇 (hào qí) - "curious"
- 神奇 (shén qí) - "magical, miraculous"
- 奇迹 (qí jì) - "miracle"
- 奇妙 (qí miào) - "wonderful, marvelous"

**💡 Special Notes:**

奇 is commonly used to describe things that are unusual, strange, or remarkable. It appears
frequently in compound words and is essential vocabulary for expressing curiosity, wonder, or
describing unusual situations.

**📍 Usage Context:**

The character 奇 often carries a positive connotation when describing something remarkable or
wonderful, though it can also simply mean "strange" or "odd" in neutral contexts.
