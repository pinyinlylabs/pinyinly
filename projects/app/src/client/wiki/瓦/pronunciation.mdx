**🗣️ Pronunciation of 瓦 (wǎ)**

- **Pinyin:** wǎ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like examining a tile

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "want"
- **ǎ** sounds like **"ah"** in "father" but with third tone — dip down and rise up
- **wǎ** sounds like **"wah"** with thoughtful dip-then-rise

**🎯 Mastering the "w" sound:**

The **w** in Chinese is:

1. **Round the lips** — like making an "oo" shape
2. **Quick glide** — don't linger on the "w" sound
3. **Similar to English** — but move quickly to the vowel
4. **Smooth transition** — flows naturally into the following vowel

**🎯 Mastering the "ǎ" final:**

The **ǎ** with third tone:

1. **"a"** like "ah" in "father" — open and relaxed
2. **Third tone pattern** — fall then rise, like "uh-huh"
3. **Dip in middle** — voice goes down then comes back up
4. **Contemplative quality** — like examining something carefully

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is **fall-then-rise**:

Say it like you're **examining** — "wǎ..." with that thoughtful dip and rise

**📝 Practice words:**

- 瓦 (wǎ) - "tile; pottery"
- 瓦片 (wǎ piàn) - "roof tile"
- 砖瓦 (zhuān wǎ) - "bricks and tiles"
- 瓦房 (wǎ fáng) - "tile-roofed house"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of examining a ceramic **tile** in your hands — "wǎ..." with that third tone captures the
careful, contemplative inspection of the craftsmanship and quality.
