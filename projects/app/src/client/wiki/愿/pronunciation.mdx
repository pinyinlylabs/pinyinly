**🗣️ Pronunciation of 愿 (yuàn)**

- **Pinyin:** yuàn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like making a sincere promise

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **yu** like **"you"** but quicker and more compressed
- **àn** sounds like **"ahn"** with a sharp falling tone and nasal ending
- **yuàn** sounds like **"you-ahn"** with a sincere, decisive drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're making a heartfelt promise or sincere wish: **"yuàn!"** — that firm downward tone
expresses genuine intention and sincerity.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 愿 (yuàn) - "sincere; willing; to wish"
- 愿意 (yuàn yì) - "willing; to be willing to"
- 愿望 (yuàn wàng) - "wish; desire; aspiration"
- 心愿 (xīn yuàn) - "heartfelt wish"
- 志愿 (zhì yuàn) - "volunteer; aspiration"
- 情愿 (qíng yuàn) - "would rather; prefer to"

**🎭 Usage Note:**

愿 (yuàn) expresses willingness, sincerity, and genuine desire. It often appears in contexts
involving promises, wishes, or voluntary actions, carrying a sense of heartfelt commitment.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of the firm, decisive tone you use when making a sincere promise — **"yuàn!"** — that
committed falling tone captures the genuine nature of a heartfelt wish!
