## Core Meaning & Usage

买 represents **the act of acquiring goods or services through payment**. It's used:

1. **General purchasing**: 买东西 (mǎi dōngxi) - "buy things"
2. **Food shopping**: 买菜 (mǎi cài) - "buy groceries"
3. **Major purchases**: 买房子 (mǎi fángzi) - "buy a house"
4. **Services**: 买票 (mǎi piào) - "buy tickets"

## Examples

- **买书** (mǎi shū) - "buy books"
- **买衣服** (mǎi yīfu) - "buy clothes"
- **买单** (mǎi dān) - "pay the bill"
- **购买** (gòumǎi) - "purchase" (formal)
- **买不起** (mǎi bù qǐ) - "can't afford to buy"

## Cultural Context

买 reflects the fundamental role of commerce in Chinese society. The concept connects to traditional
market culture where bargaining and relationship-building are important aspects of purchasing. The
phrase "买卖" (mǎimài) combines buying and selling, emphasizing that commerce is about mutual
exchange and relationship. In modern China, 买 has expanded to include online shopping culture,
reflecting the rapid digitization of commerce and the importance of consumption in economic
development.
