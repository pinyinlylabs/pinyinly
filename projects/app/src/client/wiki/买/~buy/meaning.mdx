To acquire (something) in exchange for money.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                                   |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | mǎi                                    |
| Core meaning   | buy; purchase; acquire through payment |
| Part of speech | verb                                   |
| Tone           | mǎi (3rd)                              |

## Character Breakdown

买 is a simplified character representing the concept of acquisition through exchange.

| Component | Meaning                                                    |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **买**    | Simplified from 買: originally 罒 (net) + 贝 (shell/money) |

The traditional form 買 showed a net (罒) capturing shells (贝), which were used as ancient
currency, representing the act of capturing goods through payment.

## Mnemonic

Think of 买 as **"catching what you want with money"**:

- The top part (乛) looks like a hand reaching for something
- The bottom part resembles a container or basket for purchases
- Picture yourself at a market, reaching out to acquire goods
- Your money "catches" the items you desire
- The exchange of payment for products
- The satisfaction of obtaining something valuable

This creates the image: **using money to capture desired goods**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

买 represents **the act of acquiring goods or services through payment**. It's used:

1. **General purchasing**: 买东西 (mǎi dōngxi) - "buy things"
2. **Food shopping**: 买菜 (mǎi cài) - "buy groceries"
3. **Major purchases**: 买房子 (mǎi fángzi) - "buy a house"
4. **Services**: 买票 (mǎi piào) - "buy tickets"

## Examples

- **买书** (mǎi shū) - "buy books"
- **买衣服** (mǎi yīfu) - "buy clothes"
- **买单** (mǎi dān) - "pay the bill"
- **购买** (gòumǎi) - "purchase" (formal)
- **买不起** (mǎi bù qǐ) - "can't afford to buy"

## Cultural Context

买 reflects the fundamental role of commerce in Chinese society. The concept connects to traditional
market culture where bargaining and relationship-building are important aspects of purchasing. The
phrase "买卖" (mǎimài) combines buying and selling, emphasizing that commerce is about mutual
exchange and relationship. In modern China, 买 has expanded to include online shopping culture,
reflecting the rapid digitization of commerce and the importance of consumption in economic
development.
