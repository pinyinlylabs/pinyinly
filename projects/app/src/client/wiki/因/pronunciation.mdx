**🗣️ Pronunciation of 因 (yīn)**

- **Pinyin:** yīn
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Cause"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **īn** sounds like **"een"** in "seen" but held steady and high
- **yīn** sounds like **"yeen"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're stating a fact steadily: **"yīn..."** — that's the tone pattern of **yīn**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 因 (yīn) - "cause; reason; because of"
- 因为 (yīn wèi) - "because"
- 因此 (yīn cǐ) - "therefore"
- 原因 (yuán yīn) - "reason; cause"
- 因素 (yīn sù) - "factor; element"

**💡 Special Notes:**

因 represents cause, reason, or the basis for something happening. It's most commonly seen
in 因为 (yīn wèi), meaning "because," which is essential for expressing causality in Chinese. The
character is fundamental for logical reasoning and explaining why things happen.
