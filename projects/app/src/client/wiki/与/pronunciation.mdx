**🗣️ Pronunciation of 与 (yǔ)**

- **Pinyin:** yǔ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **ǔ** sounds like **"oo"** in "book", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **yǔ** sounds like **"yoo"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking or being thoughtful: **"yǔ..."** — that's the tone pattern of **yǔ**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 与 (yǔ) - "and; with; together with"
- 与其 (yǔ qí) - "rather than"
- 参与 (cān yǔ) - "to participate"
- 给与 (jǐ yǔ) - "to give"

**💡 Special Notes:**

与 is a formal conjunction meaning "and" or "with," commonly used in written Chinese. In spoken
Chinese, 和 (hé) is more frequently used for "and." 与 appears often in formal documents,
literature, and academic writing to connect nouns or phrases.
