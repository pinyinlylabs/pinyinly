**🗣️ Pronunciation of 宀 (mián)**

- **Pinyin:** mián
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "moon"
- **ián** sounds like **"yen"** in "Japanese yen", but with second tone → rising upward
- **mián** sounds like **"myen?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking "roof?" with curiosity: **"mián?"** — that's the rising tone pattern of
**mián**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 宀 (mián) - "roof radical"
- 宀 appears in characters like 家 (jiā) - "family/home"
- 宀 appears in characters like 安 (ān) - "peaceful"
- 宀 appears in characters like 室 (shì) - "room"

**📍 Cultural Note:**

宀 is a **radical** (component) that represents a roof or shelter. It's not used as a standalone
word in modern Chinese, but appears as a component in many characters related to homes, buildings,
and shelter.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Roof** — the rising second tone is like the peaked shape of a traditional **roof**!
