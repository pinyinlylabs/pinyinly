## Core Meaning & Usage

害 represents **causing harm, injury, or damage to people, things, or situations**. It's used:

1. **Direct harm**: 害人 (hài rén) - "harm people"
2. **Health effects**: 有害 (yǒuhài) - "harmful; detrimental"
3. **Environmental**: 害虫 (hàichóng) - "pest; harmful insect"
4. **Consequences**: 害怕 (hàipà) - "fear; be afraid" (literally "fear harm")

## Examples

- **有害** (yǒuhài) - "harmful; detrimental"
- **害怕** (hàipà) - "fear; be afraid"
- **害虫** (hàichóng) - "pest; harmful insect"
- **伤害** (shānghài) - "injure; wound; harm"
- **害处** (hàichu) - "harm; disadvantage"

## Cultural Context

害 in Chinese culture represents not just physical harm but also moral and social damage. The
concept includes harm to relationships, reputation, and community harmony. Traditional Chinese
medicine and philosophy emphasize preventing 害 through balance and proper care, reflecting a
holistic view of harm that extends beyond just physical injury to include emotional and spiritual
well-being.
