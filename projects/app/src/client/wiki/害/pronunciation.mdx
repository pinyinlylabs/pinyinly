**🗣️ Pronunciation of 害 (hài)**

- **Pinyin:** hài
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **h** like **"h"** in "hat"
- **ài** sounds like **"eye"** but with fourth tone → sharp downward drop
- **hài** sounds like **"hi!"** with a sharp falling tone

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like expressing alarm about danger: **"hài!"** — that sharp drop conveys the seriousness of
**harm**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 害 (hài) - "to harm; injure; damage"
- 害怕 (hài pà) - "to be afraid; scared"
- 害羞 (hài xiū) - "to be shy; bashful"
- 伤害 (shāng hài) - "to hurt; harm"
- 危害 (wēi hài) - "to endanger; harm"
- 厉害 (lì hai) - "formidable; serious; severe"

**📍 Usage Note:**

害 can mean physical harm, emotional damage, or being afraid. The context determines the specific
meaning, but it generally relates to negative effects or fear.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Injure** — the sharp falling fourth tone is like the sudden shock of getting **hurt**!
