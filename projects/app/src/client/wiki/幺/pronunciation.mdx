**🗣️ Pronunciation of 幺 (yāo)**

- **Pinyin:** yāo
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Yaoo"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yow"
- **āo** sounds like **"ow"** in "cow" but held steady and high
- **yāo** sounds like **"yow"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip (First tone: ˉ)**

The **first tone** is **high and flat**, like holding a steady note:

Say it like describing something **tiny**: **"yāo..."** — that steady, descriptive tone is the
**first tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 幺 (yāo) - "tiny; small; youngest"
- 幺妹 (yāo mèi) - "youngest sister"
- 幺儿 (yāo ér) - "youngest child"
- 幺爸 (yāo bà) - "youngest uncle"

**📍 Cultural Note:**

幺 is often used in dialectal contexts to refer to the youngest or smallest in a family, especially
in regions like Sichuan.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **幺** as describing something **tiny** with that steady, matter-of-fact **first tone**!
