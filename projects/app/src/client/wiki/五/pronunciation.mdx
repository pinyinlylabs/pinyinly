**🗣️ Pronunciation of 五 (wǔ)**

- **Pinyin:** wǔ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **ǔ** sounds like **"oo"** in "book", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **wǔ** sounds like **"woo"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is a **fall–then-rise** tone, like being thoughtful:

Say it like you're counting carefully: **"wǔ..."** — that thoughtful dip-and-rise is the **third
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 五 (wǔ) - "five"
- 五月 (wǔ yuè) - "May"
- 五十 (wǔ shí) - "fifty"
- 星期五 (xīng qī wǔ) - "Friday"
- 五分钟 (wǔ fēn zhōng) - "five minutes"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **五** as holding up five fingers while thinking — that's the **third tone** contemplative
quality!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

五 is an important number in Chinese culture, appearing in concepts like 五行 (wǔ xíng) - "five
elements" and traditional medicine.
