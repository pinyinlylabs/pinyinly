**🗣️ Pronunciation of 读 (dú)**

- **Pinyin:** dú
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"doo?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "do" but **unaspirated** — no puff of air, softer than English "d"
- **ú** sounds like **"oo"** in "zoo" but with a rising tone
- **dú** sounds like **"doo?"** with a questioning rise

**🔊 Key Sound Tips:**

The **d** in Chinese is **unaspirated**:

1. **No puff of air** — hold your hand in front of your mouth and feel NO air when you say it
2. **Softer than English "d"** — more like the "d" in "ladder" than in "do"
3. **Voice immediately** — start the vocal cords vibrating right away
4. **Tongue tip touches** the roof of your mouth gently

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Start **low and rise up** like you're asking a question: **"dú?"**

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 读 (dú) - "read; study"
- 读书 (dú shū) - "read books; study"
- 读者 (dú zhě) - "reader"
- 朗读 (lǎng dú) - "read aloud"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think **"do?"** with that rising questioning tone — like asking "do you read?" when encouraging
someone to study!
