**🗣️ Pronunciation of 亠 (tóu)**

- **Pinyin:** tóu
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **t** like **"t"** in "top"
- **óu** sounds like **"oh"** but with a rising tone
- **tóu** sounds like **"toe?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ˊ)**

The **second tone** is a **rising** tone, like something on top going upward:

Say it like you're pointing upward to something: **"tóu?"** — that's the upward energy of **tóu**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 亠 (tóu) - "lid; top cover" (radical component)

**💡 Special Notes:**

亠 is primarily used as a radical component in Chinese characters, representing a **lid** or **top
cover**. It appears at the top of characters like:

- 京 (jīng) - "capital"
- 亮 (liàng) - "bright"
- 高 (gāo) - "tall/high"
- 夜 (yè) - "night"

**📍 Visual Connection:**

The radical 亠 looks like a simple roof or lid covering something below, which matches its meaning
of "lid" or "top cover"!
