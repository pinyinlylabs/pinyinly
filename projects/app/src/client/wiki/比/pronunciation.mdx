**🗣️ Pronunciation of 比 (bǐ)**

- **Pinyin:** bǐ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **b** like **"b"** in "bee"
- **ǐ** sounds like **"ee"** in "see", but with the **third tone** → dip down and rise up
- **bǐ** sounds like **"bee"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is a **fall–then-rise** tone, like being thoughtful:

Say it like you're comparing two things: **"bǐ..."** — that contemplative dip-and-rise is the
**third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 比 (bǐ) - "compare; than"
- 比较 (bǐ jiào) - "to compare; relatively"
- 比如 (bǐ rú) - "for example"
- 比赛 (bǐ sài) - "competition; match"
- 对比 (duì bǐ) - "contrast; comparison"
- 比方 (bǐ fāng) - "analogy; example"

**💡 Special Notes:**

比 is essential for making comparisons in Chinese. It's used in the pattern "A 比 B + adjective" to
mean "A is more [adjective] than B," such as 他比我高 (tā bǐ wǒ gāo) - "He is taller than me."
