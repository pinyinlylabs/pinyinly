**🗣️ Pronunciation of 龠 (yuè)**

- **Pinyin:** yuè
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **y** like **"y"** in "yes"
- **uè** sounds like **"weh"** but with fourth tone → sharp fall
- **yuè** sounds like **"yweh!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Start high and drop sharply, like giving a firm command: **"yuè!"** — that's the tone pattern of
**yuè**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 龠 (yuè) - "flute; ancient wind instrument"
- 音樂 (yīn yuè) - "music" (note: different character 樂)
- 樂器 (yuè qì) - "musical instrument" (note: different character 樂)

**💡 Special Note:**

龠 is an ancient character representing a type of flute. It's rarely used in modern Chinese but
appears in classical texts and as a radical component.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of the sharp, decisive sound when a flute player **cuts off** a note abruptly — that sharp
falling **"yuè!"**
