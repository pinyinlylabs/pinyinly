**🗣️ Pronunciation of 鬲 (lì)**

- **Pinyin:** lì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **l** like **"l"** in "light"
- **ì** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **lì** sounds like **"lee!"** with a sharp falling pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone: Say it like announcing something important:
**"lì!"** — that's the firm, decisive tone pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 鬲 (lì) - "cauldron; ancient cooking vessel"
- 隔 (gé) - "separate" (contains 鬲 as a component)

**💡 Special Notes:**

鬲 is an **ancient character** referring to a three-legged bronze cooking vessel used in ancient
China. It's rarely used as a standalone character in modern Chinese, but appears as a radical or
component in other characters.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of the **solid, firm** bronze cauldron standing strong — that matches the decisive, falling
tone of 鬲!
