**🗣️ Pronunciation of 癶 (bō)**

- **Pinyin:** bō
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **b** like **"b"** in "bee"
- **ō** sounds like **"o"** in "more", but held steady and high
- **bō** sounds like **"bow"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're stating something with confidence: **"bō"** — keep that high, steady tone.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 癶 (bō) - "split; dotted tent" (radical)
- 登 (dēng) - "climb; ascend" (contains 癶)
- 發 (fā) - "emit; issue" (traditional form, contains 癶)

**💡 Memory Tip:**

癶 represents "split" or "dotted tent" — the steady first tone sounds like confidently setting up
camp!

**📝 Note:**

癶 is primarily used as a radical component in characters related to climbing, ascending, or
splitting. It's not commonly used independently in modern Chinese.
