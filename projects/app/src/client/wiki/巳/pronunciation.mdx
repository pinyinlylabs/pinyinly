**🗣️ Pronunciation of 巳 (sì)**

- **Pinyin:** sì
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"See!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **s** like **"s"** in "see"
- **ì** sounds like **"ee"** with a sharp falling tone
- **sì** sounds like **"see!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) starts **high** and drops **fast**:

Say it like you're announcing the time: **"sì!"** — that's the decisive energy of **sì**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 巳 (sì) - "9 to 11 AM" (traditional Chinese time period)
- 巳时 (sì shí) - "9-11 AM time period"
- 丁巳 (dīng sì) - used in traditional calendar systems
- 己巳 (jǐ sì) - used in traditional calendar systems

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **巳** as the mid-morning time — that sharp **fourth tone** marks the definitive time
period!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

巳 is one of the 12 earthly branches (地支) in the traditional Chinese calendar system,
corresponding to the snake in the zodiac and the time period 9-11 AM.

**📍 Similar Characters:**

- **己** (jǐ) - "oneself" (3rd tone)
- **已** (yǐ) - "already" (3rd tone)
