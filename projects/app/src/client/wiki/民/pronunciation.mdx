**🗣️ Pronunciation of 民 (mín)**

- **Pinyin:** mín
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "mean"
- **ín** sounds like **"een"** in "seen" but with a **rising** tone
- **mín** sounds like **"meen?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ˊ)**

The **second tone** is **rising**, like asking a question:

Say it like you're asking about the people: **"mín?"** — that questioning rise is the **second
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 民 (mín) - "people; citizens"
- 人民 (rén mín) - "people; citizens"
- 民族 (mín zú) - "ethnic group; nationality"
- 民主 (mín zhǔ) - "democracy"
- 居民 (jū mín) - "residents"
- 农民 (nóng mín) - "farmer; peasant"
- 公民 (gōng mín) - "citizen"

**💡 Special Notes:**

民 is a fundamental concept in Chinese culture and politics, referring to the people or citizens of
a country. It's commonly used in political, social, and cultural contexts to discuss citizenship,
ethnicity, and governance.
