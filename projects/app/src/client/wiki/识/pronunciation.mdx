**🗣️ Pronunciation of 识 (shí)**

- **Pinyin:** shí
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"shee?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "shoe" but with your **tongue curled back** further toward the roof of the
  mouth
- **í** sounds like **"ee"** in "see" but with a rising tone
- **shí** sounds like **"shee?"** with a questioning rise

**🎯 Mastering the "sh" sound:**

The **sh** in Chinese is **different** from English "sh":

1. **Curl your tongue back** — tip pointing toward the roof of your mouth
2. **Keep tongue sides touching** your upper teeth
3. **Make it deeper** — more throaty than English "sh"
4. **Think retroflex** — tongue curled back like you're making an "r" shape but saying "sh"

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Start **low and rise up** like you're asking a question: **"shí?"**

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 识 (shí) - "recognize; know"
- 知识 (zhī shí) - "knowledge"
- 认识 (rèn shí) - "recognize; know (someone)"
- 常识 (cháng shí) - "common sense"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think **"she?"** but with that curled-back Chinese "sh" sound and a rising tone — like asking "she?"
in recognition!
