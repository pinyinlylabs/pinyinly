**🗣️ Pronunciation of 万 (wàn)**

- **Pinyin:** wàn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **àn** sounds like **"ahn"** with a sharp falling tone
- **wàn** sounds like **"wahn!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're making a firm statement: **"wàn!"** — that's the tone pattern of **wàn**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 万 (wàn) - "ten thousand"
- 千万 (qiān wàn) - "ten million"
- 万分 (wàn fēn) - "extremely"
- 万岁 (wàn suì) - "long live"

**💡 Special Notes:**

万 is a fundamental number in Chinese representing 10,000. It's used as a basic unit for large
numbers, similar to how "thousand" functions in English. The character is essential for
understanding Chinese number systems and appears frequently in both formal and casual contexts.
