**🗣️ Pronunciation of 将**

将 has **two main pronunciations** depending on meaning:

**📍 jiāng (first tone) - "will" (future tense)**

- **Pinyin:** jiāng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **iāng** sounds like **"yahng"** with a steady high tone
- **jiāng** sounds like **"jyahng"** with a steady high pitch

**📍 jiàng (fourth tone) - "general" (military)**

- **Pinyin:** jiàng
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **j** like **"j"** in "jeep"
- **iàng** sounds like **"yahng"** but with a sharp falling tone
- **jiàng** sounds like **"jyahng!"** with a sharp drop

**📝 Common Examples:**

**jiāng (first tone - "will"):**

- 将 (jiāng) - "will"
- 将来 (jiāng lái) - "future"
- 将要 (jiāng yào) - "about to"

**jiàng (fourth tone - "general"):**

- 将军 (jiàng jūn) - "general"
- 将领 (jiàng lǐng) - "commander"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

"Will" is smooth and forward-looking (first tone), "general" is sharp and commanding (fourth tone)!
