**🗣️ Pronunciation of 艮 (gèn)**

- **Pinyin:** gèn
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a firm command to stop

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **g** like **"g"** in "go"
- **èn** sounds like **"un"** in "fun", but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **gèn** sounds like **"gun"** with a decisive falling tone

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it firmly, like commanding something to halt: **"gèn!"** — sharp and final.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 艮 (gèn) - "stopping; mountain (in bagua)"
- 艮位 (gèn wèi) - "northeast direction (in feng shui)"

**💡 Cultural Note:**

艮 (gèn) is one of the eight trigrams (bagua) in Chinese philosophy, representing "mountain" and
"stopping/stillness." It's primarily used in traditional Chinese contexts like I Ching, feng shui,
and Taoist philosophy. In everyday modern Chinese, this character is rarely used independently.
