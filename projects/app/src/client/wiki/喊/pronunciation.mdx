**🗣️ Pronunciation of 喊 (hǎn)**

- **Pinyin:** hǎn
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **h** like **"h"** in "hat"
- **ǎn** sounds like **"ahn"** but with the **third tone** → dip down and rise up
- **hǎn** sounds like **"hahn"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're calling out thoughtfully: **"hǎn..."** — that's the tone pattern of **hǎn**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 喊 (hǎn) - "to shout; to yell"
- 喊叫 (hǎn jiào) - "to shout and scream"
- 大喊 (dà hǎn) - "to shout loudly"
- 呼喊 (hū hǎn) - "to call out"
- 喊声 (hǎn shēng) - "shouting voice"

**💡 Special Notes:**

喊 specifically means to shout or call out loudly, often to get someone's attention or to express
something urgently. It's different from 说 (shuō) which is normal speaking - 喊 implies volume and
intensity in vocal expression.
