**🗣️ Pronunciation of 待 (dài)**

- **Pinyin:** dài
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Die!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **d** like **"d"** in "day"
- **ài** sounds like **"eye"** but with fourth tone → sharp fall
- **dài** sounds like **"die"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're firmly telling someone to **wait**: **"dài!"** — that's the decisive tone pattern
of **dài**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 待 (dài) - "to wait; to treat"
- 等待 (děng dài) - "to wait for"
- 对待 (duì dài) - "to treat; to deal with"
- 接待 (jiē dài) - "to receive; to welcome"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **"die"** with a sharp fall — when telling someone to **wait**, you often use a firm,
decisive tone, just like the fourth tone's pattern!
