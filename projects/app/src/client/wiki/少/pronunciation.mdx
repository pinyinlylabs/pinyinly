**🗣️ Pronunciation of 少**

少 has **two main pronunciations** depending on meaning:

**📍 shǎo (third tone) - "a few" (small quantity)**

- **Pinyin:** shǎo
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "shoe"
- **ǎo** sounds like **"ow"** in "how" but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **shǎo** sounds like **"show"** with a dip-then-rise

**📍 shào (fourth tone) - "young" (age)**

- **Pinyin:** shào
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **sh** like **"sh"** in "shoe"
- **ào** sounds like **"ow"** in "how" but with a sharp falling tone
- **shào** sounds like **"show!"** with a sharp drop

**📝 Common Examples:**

**shǎo (third tone - "a few"):**

- 少 (shǎo) - "a few"
- 很少 (hěn shǎo) - "very few"
- 不少 (bù shǎo) - "quite a few"

**shào (fourth tone - "young"):**

- 少年 (shào nián) - "youth"
- 青少年 (qīng shào nián) - "teenager"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

"Few" has uncertainty (third tone dip), "young" is definitive (fourth tone drop)!
