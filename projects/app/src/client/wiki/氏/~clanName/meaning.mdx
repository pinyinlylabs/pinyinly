A common character used to represent a clan or family name.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                       |
| -------------- | -------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | shì                        |
| Core meaning   | clan; family name; surname |
| Part of speech | noun                       |
| Tone           | 4th tone                   |

## Visual Breakdown

氏 represents **family lineage and clan identity**:

| Component | Meaning         | Contribution to 氏              |
| --------- | --------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **一**    | horizontal line | Shows foundation/base of family |
| **丿**    | slanting stroke | Shows branching lineage         |

## Character Analysis

### 氏 (shì) - clan/family name

- **Structure**: Simple pictograph showing branching from a base
- **Etymology**: Originally depicted family lineage branching from common ancestor
- **Visual**: A family tree with base foundation and extending branches
- **Evolution**: Became suffix for surnames and clan names

## Mnemonic

Think of **氏** as a simplified **family tree** with a horizontal **一** (base) representing the
founding ancestor and the slanting stroke **丿** showing how the family branches out into different
lineages and generations.

## Usage Examples

| Context        | Example  | Translation                  |
| -------------- | -------- | ---------------------------- |
| Surnames       | 王氏家族 | The Wang clan/family         |
| Historical     | 孔氏后代 | Descendants of the Kong clan |
| Formal address | 李氏女士 | Ms. Li (formal)              |
| Genealogy      | 氏族记录 | Clan records                 |

## Grammar Patterns

- **姓氏**: Surname/family name
- **[Surname] + 氏**: The [Surname] clan/family
- **氏族**: Clan/tribal group
- **某氏**: A certain family/clan

## Cultural Context

氏 represents the fundamental importance of family lineage and ancestral identity in Chinese
culture. It reflects the Confucian emphasis on family relationships and the continuity of
bloodlines. In traditional China, one's 氏 determined social status, marriage possibilities, and
spiritual obligations to ancestors, making it central to personal and social identity.
