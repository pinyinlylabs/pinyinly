**🗣️ Pronunciation of 情 (qíng)**

- **Pinyin:** qíng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like expressing emotion with enthusiasm

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **q** like **"ch"** in "cheese" but **sharper** and more **aspirated**
- **íng** sounds like **"eeng"** with a rising tone and nasal ending
- **qíng** sounds like **"cheeng"** with an enthusiastic, emotional rise

**🎯 Mastering the "q" sound:**

The **q** in Chinese is **different** from English:

1. **Start with "ch"** like in "cheese"
2. **Add strong breath** — more air puff than English "ch"
3. **Keep it sharp** — crisp and clean articulation

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're getting excited about something emotional: **"qíng!"** — that upward inflection
captures the rising feeling of emotion.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 情 (qíng) - "feeling; emotion; affection"
- 情况 (qíng kuàng) - "situation; circumstances"
- 爱情 (ài qíng) - "love; romance"
- 心情 (xīn qíng) - "mood; frame of mind"
- 情感 (qíng gǎn) - "emotion; sentiment"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of how your voice rises when you talk about something you feel passionate about — **"qíng!"**
— that emotional lift is perfect for the second tone!
