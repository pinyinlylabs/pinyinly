**🗣️ Pronunciation of 戈 (gē)**

- **Pinyin:** gē
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady, sharp command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **g** like **"g"** in "go"
- **ē** sounds like **"uh"** in "duh" but held steady and high
- **gē** sounds like **"guh"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're calling out a sharp military command: **"gē!"** — that steady high tone matches
the weapon-like nature of a halberd.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 戈 (gē) - "halberd; ancient Chinese weapon"
- 戈壁 (gē bì) - "Gobi Desert"
- 干戈 (gān gē) - "weapons of war; warfare"

**🎭 Usage Note:**

戈 (gē) originally referred to an ancient Chinese polearm weapon (halberd). Today it's mainly seen
in compound words and place names. As a radical (戈部), it appears in characters related to weapons,
war, and conflict.

**🔤 As a Radical:**

戈 appears as a component in many characters:

- 我 (wǒ) - "I; me"
- 战 (zhàn) - "war; battle"
- 或 (huò) - "or; perhaps"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of a soldier sharply calling out with a weapon in hand — **"gē!"** — that firm, high tone
captures the authority and sharpness of an ancient halberd!
