**🗣️ Pronunciation of 苦 (kǔ)**

- **Pinyin:** kǔ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **k** like **"k"** in "key"
- **ǔ** sounds like **"oo"** in "book", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **kǔ** sounds like **"koo"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Third tone: ˇ)**

The **third tone** is a **fall–then-rise** tone, like being thoughtful:

Say it like you're contemplating something bitter: **"kǔ..."** — that thoughtful dip-and-rise is the
**third tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 苦 (kǔ) - "bitter"
- 辛苦 (xīn kǔ) - "hardship, hard work"
- 痛苦 (tòng kǔ) - "painful, suffering"
- 苦瓜 (kǔ guā) - "bitter gourd"
- 吃苦 (chī kǔ) - "to endure hardship"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **苦** as the contemplative moment when tasting something bitter — that thoughtful reaction
matches the **third tone**!

**📍 Cultural Note:**

苦 represents an important concept in Chinese philosophy and medicine, often associated with balance
(as in bitter foods being healthy) and the value of enduring hardship.
