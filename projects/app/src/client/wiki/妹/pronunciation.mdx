**🗣️ Pronunciation of 妹 (mèi)**

- **Pinyin:** mèi
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **m** like **"m"** in "may"
- **èi** sounds like **"ay"** in "may", but with fourth tone → sharp falling pitch
- **mèi** sounds like **"may!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're calling out confidently: **"mèi!"** — that's the decisive drop of the **fourth
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 妹 (mèi) - "younger sister" (usually in compounds)
- 妹妹 (mèi mei) - "younger sister"
- 小妹 (xiǎo mèi) - "little sister"
- 妹子 (mèi zi) - "girl, young woman" (colloquial)
- 表妹 (biǎo mèi) - "younger female cousin"
- 堂妹 (táng mèi) - "younger paternal female cousin"

**💡 Special Notes:**

妹 refers specifically to a younger sister and uses the "woman" radical (女) combined with 未. In
Chinese culture, the distinction between older and younger siblings is important, so 妹妹 (younger
sister) is different from 姐姐 (older sister).

**📍 Family Hierarchy:**

- 姐姐 (jiě jie) - older sister
- 妹妹 (mèi mei) - younger sister
- The age relationship is always specified in Chinese family terms

**💡 Cultural Note:**

In Chinese families, birth order determines the specific term used, making 妹妹 an essential family
vocabulary word.
