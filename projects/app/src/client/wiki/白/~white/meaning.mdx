White; the color of snow and milk; representing purity, cleanliness, and simplicity.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                   |
| -------------- | ---------------------- |
| Pinyin         | bái                    |
| Core meaning   | white; pure; clear     |
| Part of speech | adjective, noun        |
| Tone           | second tone (rising)   |

## Visual Breakdown

白 represents **a white acorn or nut**, symbolizing purity and simplicity.

| Component | Visual Description                                          |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **白**    | Originally an acorn shape, representing something naturally white |
| **丶**    | Small dot showing the pointed tip or highlight              |

## Mnemonic

Think of 白 as **a pure white acorn gleaming in sunlight**:

- The main body shows the natural white color of certain nuts or seeds
- The small dot (丶) represents the highlight or gleam of pure white
- Like a pristine white acorn that catches the light
- Shows natural whiteness without any artificial coloring
- Represents purity in its most natural form

This creates the image: **natural whiteness shining with pure light**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

白 represents **whiteness, purity, and clarity**. It's used:

1. **Basic color**: 白色 (bái sè) - "white color"
2. **Objects**: 白雪 (bái xuě) - "white snow"
3. **Purity**: 清白 (qīng bái) - "innocent; pure"
4. **Blank/empty**: 白纸 (bái zhǐ) - "blank paper"

## Examples

- **白天** (bái tiān) - "daytime" (literally "white day")
- **白云** (bái yún) - "white clouds"
- **白发** (bái fà) - "white/gray hair"
- **白菜** (bái cài) - "Chinese cabbage" (literally "white vegetable")
- **白米** (bái mǐ) - "white rice"
- **黑白** (hēi bái) - "black and white"

## Cultural Significance

白 has complex meanings in Chinese culture:

**Positive Associations:**
- **纯洁** (chún jié) - Purity and innocence
- **清洁** (qīng jié) - Cleanliness and hygiene
- **简单** (jiǎn dān) - Simplicity and minimalism
- **诚实** (chéng shí) - Honesty and truthfulness

**Traditional Associations:**
- **丧事** (sāng shì) - Mourning and funerals (white clothing)
- **素食** (sù shí) - Plain/vegetarian food
- **朴素** (pǔ sù) - Plain and unadorned style

## Mourning and Rituals

白 in traditional ceremonies:

- **白花** (bái huā) - "white flowers" (funeral arrangements)
- **白布** (bái bù) - "white cloth" (mourning garments)
- **白事** (bái shì) - "funeral affairs" (opposite of 红事 happy events)
- **披麻戴孝** - Traditional mourning dress in white/hemp

## Blank and Empty

白 indicating absence or void:

- **空白** (kōng bái) - "blank; empty space"
- **白卷** (bái juàn) - "blank exam paper"
- **白手起家** (bái shǒu qǐ jiā) - "start from nothing" (empty-handed)
- **一片空白** (yī piàn kōng bái) - "completely blank"

## Modern Usage

白 in contemporary contexts:

- **白领** (bái lǐng) - "white collar worker"
- **白酒** (bái jiǔ) - "clear liquor; Chinese spirits"
- **白糖** (bái táng) - "white sugar"
- **白金** (bái jīn) - "platinum" (literally "white gold")

## Common Expressions

- **明明白白** (míng míng bái bái) - "crystal clear; obvious"
- **白日做梦** (bái rì zuò mèng) - "daydream" (literally "dream in broad daylight")
- **白费力气** (bái fèi lì qi) - "waste effort" (do something in vain)
- **不明不白** (bù míng bù bái) - "unclear; mysterious"

## Truth and Understanding

白 expressing clarity:

- **明白** (míng bai) - "understand; clear"
- **表白** (biǎo bái) - "express feelings; confess"
- **坦白** (tǎn bái) - "frank; honest"
- **真相大白** (zhēn xiàng dà bái) - "truth comes to light"

## Wasted Effort

白 meaning "in vain":

- **白跑** (bái pǎo) - "run for nothing"
- **白等** (bái děng) - "wait in vain"
- **白忙** (bái máng) - "busy for nothing"
- **白搭** (bái dā) - "useless; no good"

## Physical Descriptions

白 describing appearance:

- **白皙** (bái xī) - "fair-skinned; pale"
- **苍白** (cāng bái) - "pale; pallid"
- **雪白** (xuě bái) - "snow white; pure white"
- **惨白** (cǎn bái) - "deathly pale"

## Grammar Functions

- **Adjective**: 白房子 (bái fáng zi) - "white house"
- **Adverb**: 白跑了 (bái pǎo le) - "ran for nothing"
- **Noun**: 这个白很纯 (zhè ge bái hěn chún) - "this white is very pure"

## Cultural Context

白 reflects important Chinese concepts:

- **阴性** (yīn xìng) - Yin energy, coolness, and receptivity
- **空性** (kōng xìng) - Buddhist concept of emptiness
- **素朴** (sù pǔ) - Daoist ideal of simplicity
- **诚恳** (chéng kěn) - Confucian value of sincerity

The color white represents the Chinese appreciation for purity, simplicity, and the beauty found in emptiness and clarity, while also carrying solemn associations with loss and mourning.
