**🗣️ Pronunciation of 苹 (píng)**

- **Pinyin:** píng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **p** like **"p"** in "ping"
- **íng** sounds like **"ing"** but with second tone → rise up
- **píng** sounds like **"ping"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ´)**

The **second tone** is a **rising** tone, like asking a question:

Say it like you're asking about an apple: **"píng?"** — that questioning rise is the **second
tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 苹 (píng) - "apple" (usually in compounds)
- 苹果 (píng guǒ) - "apple"
- 青苹果 (qīng píng guǒ) - "green apple"
- 苹果汁 (píng guǒ zhī) - "apple juice"
- 苹果树 (píng guǒ shù) - "apple tree"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **苹** as excitedly asking for an apple — that upward, eager rise matches the **second
tone**!

**📍 Usage Note:**

苹 rarely appears alone in modern Chinese; it's almost always used in the compound 苹果 (píng guǒ)
meaning "apple". The character contains the grass radical 艹, indicating its plant origin.
