**🗣️ Pronunciation of 行**

行 has **two different pronunciations** depending on its meaning:

**📍 xíng (second tone) - "to walk, to go"**

- **Pinyin:** xíng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **x** like **"sh"** in "ship"
- **íng** sounds like **"ing"** in "sing" but with a rising tone
- **xíng** sounds like **"shing?"** with a questioning rise

**📍 háng (second tone) - "row, line, profession"**

- **Pinyin:** háng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, same as above

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **h** like **"h"** in "hat"
- **áng** sounds like **"ahng"** in "song" but with a rising tone
- **háng** sounds like **"hahng?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

Both pronunciations use **second tone** (ˊ) - a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking a question or being surprised: **"Oh really?"** — that's the energy of
both **xíng** and **háng**.

**📝 Usage Guide:**

**行 (xíng) - "to walk, to go, to be okay":**

- 我可以走路去。**行**不**行**？(xíng bù xíng) - "Is it okay or not?"
- **行**人 (xíng rén) - "pedestrian"
- **行**动 (xíng dòng) - "action"

**行 (háng) - "row, line, profession":**

- 银**行** (yín háng) - "bank"
- **行**业 (háng yè) - "industry"
- 这一**行** (zhè yī háng) - "this line/row"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **xíng** as movement-related (walking, going, doing) and **háng** as static/structural
(rows, lines, professions).
