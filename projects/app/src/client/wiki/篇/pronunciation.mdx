**🗣️ Pronunciation of 篇 (piān)**

- **Pinyin:** piān
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Pyen"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **p** like **"p"** in "pen" but with a slight puff of air
- **iān** sounds like **"yen"** but with first tone → high and steady
- **piān** sounds like **"pyen"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're reciting the title of an article: **"piān..."** — that steady, formal tone.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 篇 (piān) - "article; piece (of writing)"
- 文章 (wén zhāng) - includes 篇 in meaning
- 一篇文章 (yī piān wén zhāng) - "one article"
- 诗篇 (shī piān) - "poem; verse"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **篇** as announcing an article or chapter — the first tone is steady and formal, like
reading a title aloud!
