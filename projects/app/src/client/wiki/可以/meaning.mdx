Expresses permission, ability, or possibility; can; may; able to.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                           |
| -------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Pinyin         | kěyǐ                           |
| Core meaning   | can; may; able to; permissible |
| Part of speech | auxiliary verb                 |
| Tone           | third + third tone             |

## Core Meaning & Usage

可以 expresses **permission, ability, or possibility**. It's used for:

1. **Permission**: 可以进来吗？(kěyǐ jìnlái ma?) - "may I come in?"
2. **Ability**: 我可以帮你 (wǒ kěyǐ bāng nǐ) - "I can help you"
3. **Possibility**: 可以这样做 (kěyǐ zhèyàng zuò) - "it's possible to do it this way"
4. **Approval**: 可以的 (kěyǐ de) - "it's OK/acceptable"

## Examples

- **可以试试** (kěyǐ shìshi) - "can try it"
- **不可以** (bù kěyǐ) - "cannot; not allowed"
- **可以吗？** (kěyǐ ma?) - "is it OK?; may I?"
- **当然可以** (dāngrán kěyǐ) - "of course you can"
- **可以说** (kěyǐ shuō) - "can say; it could be said"

## Nuances and Tone

可以 is versatile and polite:

- **More polite** than 能 (néng) for asking permission
- **Less formal** than 能够 (nénggòu) for expressing ability
- **Softer** than 要 (yào) for making suggestions
- **More tentative** than 会 (huì) for expressing capability

## Grammar Patterns

- **可以 + Verb**: "can/may [do action]"
- **可以不可以**: "is it OK or not?"
- **Subject + 可以 + Verb**: standard permission/ability structure

可以 is one of the most essential auxiliary verbs in Chinese for polite interaction.
