**🗣️ Pronunciation of 耳 (ěr)**

- **Pinyin:** ěr
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when listening

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ěr** sounds like **"er"** in "her", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **ěr** sounds like **"err"** with a contemplative dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're listening carefully: **"ěr..."** — that's the attentive tone pattern of **ěr**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 耳 (ěr) - "ear"
- 耳朵 (ěr duō) - "ear" (more common in spoken Chinese)
- 耳机 (ěr jī) - "headphones, earphones"
- 耳环 (ěr huán) - "earring"
- 木耳 (mù ěr) - "wood ear mushroom"

**Special Note:**

While 耳 can mean "ear" by itself, in everyday speech people usually say 耳朵 (ěr duō). 耳 appears
in many compound words related to hearing and the ear.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

When you're using your **ěr** to listen, you use that thoughtful third tone — like you're really
paying attention!
