**🗣️ Pronunciation of 尢 (wāng)**

- **Pinyin:** wāng
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "wang"
- **āng** sounds like **"ahng"** with a steady high tone
- **wāng** sounds like **"wahng"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like a steady note: **"wāng—"** — that's the tone pattern of **wāng**.

**📝 Note:**

尢 is primarily a **radical** (component) used in other characters rather than a standalone word in
modern Chinese. It represents the concept of "lame" or "crippled" and appears in characters like:

- 尴 (gān) - "awkward"
- 尤 (yóu) - "especially"
- 就 (jiù) - "then"

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 尢 (wāng) - "lame" (as a radical component)
- 尤其 (yóu qí) - "especially" (contains the 尢 radical)

**💡 Learning Tip:**

This character is mainly important for understanding the structure of other Chinese characters!
