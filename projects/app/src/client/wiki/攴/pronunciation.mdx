**🗣️ Pronunciation of 攴 (pū)**

- **Pinyin:** pū
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **p** like **"p"** in "put" (but with a slight puff of air)
- **ū** sounds like **"oo"** in "book", but held steady and high
- **pū** sounds like **"poo"** with a steady high pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (ˉ) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like singing a steady high note: **"pū~~~"** — that's the flat, steady tone of **pū**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 攴 (pū) - "tap; strike" (radical)

**💡 Special Note:**

攴 is primarily used as a **radical** (部首) in Chinese characters. It represents the action of
striking or tapping and appears in many characters related to actions performed with hands or tools,
such as:

- 攻 (gōng) - "attack"
- 收 (shōu) - "receive"
- 放 (fàng) - "release"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **"攴"** as **"poo"** held steady — like the steady sound of tapping or striking!
