**🗣️ Pronunciation of 三 (sān)**

- **Pinyin:** sān
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note: **"Sahn"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **s** like **"s"** in "see"
- **ān** sounds like **"ahn"** in "barn", but with first tone → high and steady
- **sān** sounds like **"sahn"** with a high, flat pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're holding a steady note: **"sān..."** — that's the tone pattern of **sān**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 三 (sān) - "three"
- 三月 (sān yuè) - "March"
- 三十 (sān shí) - "thirty"
- 三天 (sān tiān) - "three days"

**💡 Special Notes:**

三 is one of the most fundamental numbers in Chinese. It represents the concept of "three" and is
visually represented by three horizontal strokes. This character is essential for basic counting and
appears frequently in daily conversation, dates, and mathematical expressions.
