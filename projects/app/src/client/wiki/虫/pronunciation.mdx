**🗣️ Pronunciation of 虫 (chóng)**

- **Pinyin:** chóng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "chair" (aspirated)
- **óng** sounds like **"ong"** in "song", but with second tone → rising pitch
- **chóng** sounds like **"chong?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (´) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're asking about a bug: **"chóng?"** — that's the tone pattern of **chóng**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 虫 (chóng) - "insect/bug/worm"
- 昆虫 (kūn chóng) - "insect"
- 害虫 (hài chóng) - "pest/harmful insect"
- 毛毛虫 (máo máo chóng) - "caterpillar"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **chóng** as curiously asking about a bug you found: "What's this bug?" — the rising tone
sounds inquisitive and slightly surprised.
