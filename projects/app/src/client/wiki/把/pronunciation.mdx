**🗣️ Pronunciation of 把 (bǎ)**

- **Pinyin:** bǎ
- **Tone: Third tone** — **falling-rising** tone, like saying "uh-huh" when nodding

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **b** like **"b"** in "bat"
- **ǎ** sounds like **"ah"** in "father", but with third tone → dip down and rise up
- **bǎ** sounds like **"bah"** with a dip-then-rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **third tone** (ˇ) is a **fall–then-rise** tone:

Say it like you're thinking or being thoughtful: **"bǎ..."** — that's the tone pattern of **bǎ**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 把 (bǎ) - "handle; measure word for things with handles"
- 把手 (bǎ shǒu) - "handle; doorknob"
- 一把 (yì bǎ) - "one (measure word for things with handles)"
- 把握 (bǎ wò) - "to grasp; to hold"

**💡 Special Notes:**

把 is also commonly used as a **grammar particle** to bring the direct object before the verb:

- 把书拿来 (bǎ shū ná lái) - "bring the book here" In this usage, it's still pronounced **bǎ** with
  third tone.
