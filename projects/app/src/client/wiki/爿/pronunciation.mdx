**🗣️ Pronunciation of 爿 (qiáng)**

- **Pinyin:** qiáng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **q** like **"ch"** in "cheese" but with lips rounded
- **iáng** sounds like **"yahng"** but with a rising tone
- **qiáng** sounds like **"chyahng?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **second tone** (ˊ) is a **rising** tone:

Say it like you're questioning or asking about a bed: **"qiáng?"** — that rising intonation is the
**second tone**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 爿 (qiáng) - "bed"
- Note: 爿 is primarily used as a radical in other characters and is rarely used independently in
  modern Chinese.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **爿** as questioning where your bed is: **"qiáng?"** — that questioning rise matches the
**second tone**!

**📍 Radical Note:**

爿 appears as a radical in characters like:

- 版 (bǎn) - "edition; board"
- 片 (piàn) - "slice; piece"
