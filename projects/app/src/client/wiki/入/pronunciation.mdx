**🗣️ Pronunciation of 入 (rù)**

- **Pinyin:** rù
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **r** like **"r"** in "run" (but softer, more like "zh" sound)
- **ù** sounds like **"oo"** in "book" but with a sharp falling tone
- **rù** sounds like **"roo!"** with a sharp drop

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (`) is a **sharp falling** tone:

Say it like you're giving a firm command: **"rù!"** — that's the tone pattern of **rù**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 入 (rù) - "to enter"
- 入口 (rù kǒu) - "entrance"
- 入学 (rù xué) - "to start school; enroll"
- 进入 (jìn rù) - "to enter; go into"
- 收入 (shōu rù) - "income"

**💡 Special Notes:**

入 consistently uses the fourth tone (rù) and relates to the concept of entering, going into, or
beginning something. The character visually looks like something going downward into an opening.
