To accept as true; to have faith in; to trust; to believe.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                       |
| -------------- | -------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | xìn                        |
| Core meaning   | believe; trust; have faith |
| Part of speech | verb                       |
| Tone           | fourth tone (falling)      |

## Visual Breakdown

信 combines **person + words** to show trusted communication.

| Component | Visual Description                                       |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| **亻**    | Person radical (人) - indicates this relates to people   |
| **言**    | Speech/words (言) - represents communication or promises |

## Mnemonic

Think of 信 as **a person standing by their words**:

- The person radical (亻) shows this is about human relationships
- The speech component (言) represents spoken promises or commitments
- When a person's words are reliable, you can believe and trust them
- Like someone whose word is their bond - you have faith in what they say

## Usage Examples

- **我相信你** (wǒ xiāng xìn nǐ) - "I believe you/trust you"
- **信佛** (xìn fó) - "believe in Buddhism"
- **不信** (bù xìn) - "don't believe; disbelieve"
- **可信** (kě xìn) - "believable; trustworthy"

## Cultural Notes

信 represents a fundamental virtue in Chinese culture:

- One of the core values in Confucian ethics
- Essential for business relationships and social harmony
- Trust and reliability are highly valued character traits
- Often paired with other virtues like 义 (righteousness) and 礼 (propriety)
