**🗣️ Pronunciation of 鬯 (chàng)**

- **Pinyin:** chàng
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "change"
- **àng** sounds like **"ahng"** but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **chàng** sounds like **"chahng!"** with a sharp falling pitch

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a **sharp falling** tone: Say it like making a solemn proclamation:
**"chàng!"** — that's the ceremonial, decisive tone pattern.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 鬯 (chàng) - "sacrificial wine" (ancient ritual wine)

**💡 Special Notes:**

鬯 is an **ancient character** rarely used in modern Chinese. It refers to a fragrant wine used in
ancient Chinese religious ceremonies and sacrifices. It primarily appears in classical texts and as
a radical in some characters.

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of the **solemn, ceremonial** pouring of ritual wine — that matches the serious, falling tone
of 鬯!
