Taking action by causing change and not only reacting to change when it happens; proactive; active;
initiative; voluntary.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                         |
| -------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | zhǔ dòng                     |
| Core meaning   | proactive; taking initiative |
| Part of speech | adjective/adverb             |
| Tone           | 3rd + 4th                    |

## Usage Examples

- **主动学习** (zhǔ dòng xué xí) - "study proactively"
- **主动帮助** (zhǔ dòng bāng zhù) - "actively help; volunteer to help"
- **主动联系** (zhǔ dòng lián xì) - "take initiative to contact"
- **主动承担** (zhǔ dòng chéng dān) - "voluntarily take responsibility"
- **主动权** (zhǔ dòng quán) - "initiative; right to act first"

## Grammar Patterns

- **主动 + verb** - "proactively [verb]"
- **很主动** - "very proactive"
- **主动地...** - "proactively..."
- **主动性** - "initiative; proactiveness"

## Behavioral Contrast

- **主动** (zhǔ dòng) - proactive; taking initiative
- **被动** (bèi dòng) - passive; reactive
- **自动** (zì dòng) - automatic; self-operating
- **手动** (shǒu dòng) - manual; hand-operated

## Cultural Context

主动 reflects important Chinese values and modern expectations:

- **Educational values**: Students are encouraged to be 主动 in learning
- **Work culture**: 主动 employees are highly valued in Chinese workplaces
- **Personal development**: Being 主动 is seen as a key to success
- **Social relationships**: 主动 communication helps maintain relationships
- **Leadership qualities**: 主动 is considered essential for leadership roles
- **Modern expectations**: Contemporary Chinese society increasingly values 主动 attitudes
