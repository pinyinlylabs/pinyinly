Indicates a position that is physically lower; below; underneath; down below.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                     |
| -------------- | ------------------------ |
| Pinyin         | xiàmiàn                  |
| Core meaning   | below; underneath; under |
| Part of speech | noun, locational phrase  |
| Tone           | fourth tone + neutral    |

## Visual Breakdown

下面 combines downward direction with surface:

| Component | Visual Description                                               |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **下**    | Shows downward movement - something descending or positioned low |
| **面**    | Face/surface - represents the visible surface or side of things  |

## Mnemonic

Think of 下面 as **the surface that faces downward**:

- 下 (down/below) + 面 (face/surface) = the surface that is "down" or "below"
- Like the "underside" or bottom surface of something
- The surface of something that you can see when looking underneath
- The bottom side of an object that faces toward the ground

Together they create: **the surface/area that is positioned below**.

## Core Meaning & Usage

下面 indicates **the lower position, surface, or area beneath something**. It's used:

1. **Physical location**: 桌子下面 (zhuōzi xiàmiàn) - "under the table"
2. **In documents**: 下面写着 (xiàmiàn xiězhe) - "written below"
3. **Sequential order**: 下面是 (xiàmiàn shì) - "next is; below is"
4. **General position**: 在下面 (zài xiàmiàn) - "underneath; below"

## Examples

- **书在桌子下面** (shū zài zhuōzi xiàmiàn) - "The book is under the table"
- **楼下面** (lóu xiàmiàn) - "downstairs; below in the building"
- **地下面** (dì xiàmiàn) - "underground; beneath the ground"
- **页面下面** (yèmiàn xiàmiàn) - "at the bottom of the page"
- **山下面** (shān xiàmiàn) - "at the foot of the mountain"

## Usage Notes

下面 is more formal than just 下 (xià) and emphasizes the surface or area aspect. It's commonly used in:
- Describing locations of hidden or supported objects
- Giving directions about where to look
- Technical or formal descriptions
- Written instructions and documentation

Compare with 上面 (shàngmiàn) "above/on top" for the opposite spatial relationship.
