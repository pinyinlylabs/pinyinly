**🗣️ Pronunciation of 虍 (hū)**

- **Pinyin:** hū
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, like a steady note

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **h** like **"h"** in "hat"
- **ū** sounds like **"oo"** in "moon", but with first tone → high and steady
- **hū** sounds like **"hoo"** held steady and high

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Say it like you're making a steady sound: **"hū"** — that's the tone pattern of **hū**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 虍 (hū) - "tiger radical"
- This character is primarily used as a radical in other characters like 虎 (hǔ) "tiger"

**💡 Special Notes:**

虍 is a **radical** (component part) used in Chinese characters, particularly those related to
tigers or fierce animals. It's rarely used as a standalone character in modern Chinese, but appears
in characters like 虎 (tiger), 虚 (empty), and 虑 (worry).

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **hū** as the sound a tiger makes when breathing: "Hoo..." — the steady high tone
represents the calm before a tiger's roar.
