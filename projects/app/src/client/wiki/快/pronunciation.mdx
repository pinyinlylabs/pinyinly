**🗣️ Pronunciation of 快 (kuài)**

- **Pinyin:** kuài
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **k** like **"k"** in "key" but with a puff of air
- **uài** sounds like **"why"** but with fourth tone → sharp drop
- **kuài** sounds like **"kwy!"** with a sharp falling tone

**🎯 Mastering the "k" sound:**

The **k** in Chinese is **aspirated** (has a puff of air):

1. **Start with "k"** like in "key"
2. **Add a strong puff of air** — hold your hand in front of your mouth and feel the air
3. **Make it breathy** — like blowing out a candle
4. **Think "k" + breath**

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **fourth tone** (ˋ) is a sharp **falling** tone:

Say it like you're giving a quick command: **"kuài!"** — that's the tone pattern of **kuài**.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 快 (kuài) - "fast"
- 快乐 (kuài lè) - "happy"
- 快点儿 (kuài diǎn er) - "hurry up"
- 快要 (kuài yào) - "about to"
- 快速 (kuài sù) - "rapid, quick"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**Fast** with the sharp fourth tone — like urgently saying "Quick!" to someone!
