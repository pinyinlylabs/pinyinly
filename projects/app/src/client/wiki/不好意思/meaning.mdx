A polite way to express embarrassment or apology.

## Quick Reference

| Aspect         | Info                                   |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Pinyin         | bù hǎo yì si                           |
| Core meaning   | embarrassed; sorry; excuse me          |
| Part of speech | adjective; interjection                |
| Tone           | fourth + third + fourth + neutral tone |

## Usage Examples

- **不好意思打扰您** (bù hǎo yì si dǎrǎo nín) - "sorry to bother you"
- **真不好意思** (zhēn bù hǎo yì si) - "I'm really sorry/embarrassed"
- **有点不好意思** (yǒudiǎn bù hǎo yì si) - "a bit embarrassed"
- **不好意思，请问...** (bù hǎo yì si, qǐng wèn...) - "excuse me, may I ask..."

## Cultural Functions

不好意思 serves multiple social purposes:

- **Politeness marker**: Softening requests or interruptions
- **Apology**: For minor social mistakes or inconveniences
- **Embarrassment**: When feeling shy or awkward
- **Humility**: Downplaying achievements or compliments

## Social Contexts

- **Before requests**: 不好意思，能帮个忙吗？(excuse me, could you help?)
- **After mistakes**: 不好意思，我说错了 (sorry, I misspoke)
- **Receiving compliments**: 不好意思，哪里哪里 (I'm flattered, not really)
- **Interrupting**: 不好意思打断一下 (sorry to interrupt)

## Cultural Context

不好意思 reflects deep Chinese cultural values:

- **Face-saving**: Maintaining dignity while acknowledging awkwardness
- **Harmony**: Smoothing over social friction politely
- **Modesty**: Avoiding direct confrontation or self-promotion
- **Relationship care**: Showing consideration for others' feelings
