**🗣️ Pronunciation of 该 (gāi)**

- **Pinyin:** gāi
- **Tone: First tone** — **high and flat** tone, steady like a monotone: **"guy"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **g** like **"g"** in "go" but **unaspirated** — no puff of air, more like the "g" in "ago"
- **āi** sounds like **"eye"** but with a high, flat tone
- **gāi** sounds like **"guy"** with a steady, high pitch

**🔊 Key Sound Tips:**

The **g** in Chinese is **unaspirated**:

1. **No puff of air** — hold your hand in front of your mouth and feel NO air when you say it
2. **Softer than English "g"** — like the "g" in "ago" rather than "go"
3. **Voice immediately** — start the vocal cords vibrating right away

**🎧 Tone tip:**

The **first tone** (¯) is a **high and flat** tone:

Keep your voice **steady and high** like singing a single note: **"gāi"**

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 该 (gāi) - "should; ought to"
- 应该 (yīng gāi) - "should; ought to"
- 该死 (gāi sǐ) - "damn it" (mild curse)
- 活该 (huó gāi) - "serves you right"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think **"guy"** with a steady, high tone — like pointing at someone and saying "that guy should do
it!"
