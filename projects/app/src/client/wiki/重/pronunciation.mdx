**🗣️ Pronunciation of 重**

重 has **two different pronunciations** depending on its meaning:

**📍 zhòng (fourth tone) - "heavy, weight"**

- **Pinyin:** zhòng
- **Tone: Fourth tone** — sharp **falling** tone, like giving a command: **"Stop!"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **zh** like **"j"** in "judge" but with tongue curled back
- **òng** sounds like **"ong"** in "song" but with a falling tone
- **zhòng** sounds like **"jong!"** with a sharp drop

**📍 chóng (second tone) - "repeat, again"**

- **Pinyin:** chóng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Again?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **ch** like **"ch"** in "chair" but with tongue curled back
- **óng** sounds like **"ong"** in "song" but with a rising tone
- **chóng** sounds like **"chong?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip:**

**zhòng** (fourth tone) = **falling** ↘️ like being heavy and dropping down **chóng** (second tone)
= **rising** ↗️ like going up again to repeat

**📝 Usage Guide:**

**重 (zhòng) - "heavy, weight":**

- 这个很**重** (zhè ge hěn zhòng) - "This is very heavy"
- **重**要 (zhòng yào) - "important"
- **重**视 (zhòng shì) - "to value, attach importance to"

**重 (chóng) - "repeat, again":**

- **重**复 (chóng fù) - "to repeat"
- **重**新 (chóng xīn) - "again, anew"
- **重**来 (chóng lái) - "to start over"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

**zhòng** (heavy) = falls down ↘️ with fourth tone **chóng** (repeat) = rises up ↗️ to do it again
with second tone
