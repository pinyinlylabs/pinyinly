**🗣️ Pronunciation of 亡 (wáng)**

- **Pinyin:** wáng
- **Tone: Second tone** — **rising** tone, like asking a question: **"Really?"**

**🔤 Breakdown:**

- **w** like **"w"** in "way"
- **áng** sounds like **"ahng"** in "song" but with a rising tone
- **wáng** sounds like **"wahng?"** with a questioning rise

**🎧 Tone tip (Second tone: ˊ)**

The **second tone** is a **rising** tone, though the meaning is heavy:

Say it like you're mentioning something serious with concern: **"wáng?"** — that's the rising tone
with gravity.

**📝 Common Examples:**

- 亡 (wáng) - "death; to die; to perish"
- 死亡 (sǐ wáng) - "death"
- 亡命 (wáng míng) - "flee for one's life"
- 灭亡 (miè wáng) - "perish; destruction"
- 流亡 (liú wáng) - "exile"

**💡 Memory Tip:**

Think of **亡** as a serious topic that raises concern — that's the **second tone** rising with
weight.

**📍 Usage Note:**

亡 is primarily used in formal or literary contexts. In everyday speech, 死 (sǐ) is more commonly
used for "death." 亡 often appears in compound words and formal expressions.
